8.1 Commutativity of the point measurements

Theorem 8.1 Commutativity of the point measurements
#

Uses

  • Lemma 3.8
  • Lemma 3.12

Lean declarations

  • MIPStarRE.LDT.CommutativityPoints.commutativityPoints

Let \((\psi ,A,B,L)\) be an \((\varepsilon ,\delta ,\gamma )\)-good symmetric strategy for the \((m,q,d)\) low individual degree test. On average over independent \(u,v \sim \mathbb {F}_q^m\),

\[ (A_a^u A_b^v) \otimes I \approx _{32\gamma m} (A_b^v A_a^u) \otimes I. \]
Proof ▶

Use the diagonal-line test restricted to the full set of coordinates to compare each point measurement with the common diagonal-line answer. After converting those consistencies to state-dependent distance, commute through the shared diagonal-line measurement and transfer back.

Lemma 8.2 Commutativity after evaluation

Let \(\{ G^x\} _{x \in \mathbb {F}_q}\) be projective submeasurements in \(\mathrm{PolySub}(m,q,d)\) such that:

  1. on average over \((u,x) \sim \mathbb {F}_q^{m+1}\),

    \[ A_a^{u,x} \otimes I \simeq _\zeta I \otimes G^x_{[g(u)=a]}; \]

  2. on average over \(x \sim \mathbb {F}_q\), one has \(G_g^x \otimes I \approx _\zeta I \otimes G_g^x\);

  3. there are positive semidefinite operators \(Z^x\) with

    \[ \mathbb {E}_x \langle \psi \rvert (I-G^x) \otimes Z^x \lvert \psi \rangle \le \zeta , \qquad Z^x \ge \mathbb {E}_u A_{g(u)}^{u,x}. \]

Write \(G_a^{u,x}=G_{[g(u)=a]}^x\). Then, on average over independent \((u,x),(v,y) \sim \mathbb {F}_q^{m+1}\),

\[ G_a^{u,x} G_b^{v,y} \otimes I \approx _{48m(\gamma ^{1/2}+\zeta ^{1/2})} G_b^{v,y} G_a^{u,x} \otimes I. \]
Proof ▶

Write \(G_a^{u,x}=G^x_{[g(u)=a]}\) and expand the commutator norm into the two quartic terms

\[ \langle \psi \rvert G_b^{v,y} G_a^{u,x} G_b^{v,y} \otimes I \lvert \psi \rangle \qquad \text{and}\qquad \langle \psi \rvert G_a^{u,x} G_b^{v,y} G_a^{u,x} G_b^{v,y} \otimes I \lvert \psi \rangle . \]

For the second term, first use consistency and Lemma 3.13 to replace the rightmost evaluated \(G_b^{v,y}\) by the point projector \(A_b^{v,y}\) at cost \(2\sqrt{\zeta }\). The boundedness hypothesis lets one delete the total projector \(G^y\) at an additional cost \(\sqrt{\zeta }\), and the same two steps on the \(x\)-side produce a term containing \(A_a^{u,x}A_b^{v,y}\). Theorem 8.1 commutes these point measurements at cost \(6\sqrt{\gamma (m+1)}\), after which the same transports in reverse recover the evaluated families. Summing the eight transports gives the stated bound

\[ 48m\bigl(\gamma ^{1/2}+\zeta ^{1/2}\bigr). \]
Lemma 8.3 Normalization condition for a sandwiched family
#

Uses

  • Definition 3.3

Lean declarations

  • MIPStarRE.LDT.Commutativity.normalizationCondition

Let \(P=\{ P_a\} \) be a submeasurement and \(Q=\{ Q_b\} \) a projective submeasurement. Define \(C_{a,b}=Q_b P_a Q_b\). Then

\[ \sum _a \left(\sum _b C_{a,b}\right)^\dagger \left(\sum _b C_{a,b}\right) = \sum _a \left(\sum _b C_{a,b}\right) \left(\sum _b C_{a,b}\right)^\dagger \le I. \]
Proof ▶

Expand the squares, use the orthogonality of the projectors \(Q_b\), and sum over the submeasurement \(P\).

Let \(\{ G^x\} _{x \in \mathbb {F}_q}\) satisfy the same hypotheses as in Lemma 8.2. Then, on average over independent \(x,y \sim \mathbb {F}_q\),

\[ G_g^x G_h^y \otimes I \approx _{30m(\gamma ^{1/4}+\zeta ^{1/4}+(d/q)^{1/4})} G_h^y G_g^x \otimes I. \]
Proof ▶

Expand the commutator norm into two quartic terms. The first is reduced to the mixed overlap \(\langle \psi \rvert G\otimes G\lvert \psi \rangle \) by Lemma 3.19. For the second, introduce the intermediate expressions

\[ E_1=\mathbb {E}_{x,y}\sum _{g,h}\langle \psi \rvert G_h^y G_g^x G_h^y \otimes G_g^x \lvert \psi \rangle , \]
\[ E_2=\mathbb {E}_{u,x,y}\sum _{a,h}\langle \psi \rvert G_h^y G_{[g(u)=a]}^x G_h^y \otimes G_{[g(u)=a]}^x \lvert \psi \rangle , \]
\[ E_3=\mathbb {E}_{u,x,y}\sum _{a,h}\langle \psi \rvert G_{[g(u)=a]}^x G_h^y G_{[g(u)=a]}^x \otimes G_h^y \lvert \psi \rangle , \]
\[ E_4=\mathbb {E}_{u,v,x,y}\sum _{a,b}\langle \psi \rvert G_{[g(u)=a]}^x G_{[h(v)=b]}^y G_{[g(u)=a]}^x \otimes G_{[h(v)=b]}^y \lvert \psi \rangle . \]

Schwartz–Zippel gives \(E_1 \approx _{dm/q} E_2\) and \(E_3 \approx _{dm/q} E_4\). Between them, two applications of Lemma 3.13, each using the normalization condition from Lemma 8.3 for the sandwiched families, give \(E_2 \approx _{2\sqrt{\zeta }} E_3\). Now Lemma 8.2 commutes the evaluated operators in \(E_4\) with error

\[ \nu _{\mathrm{evaluation}}=48m\bigl(\gamma ^{1/2}+\zeta ^{1/2}\bigr), \]

so after one final transport using the strong self-consistency of the evaluated families we obtain

\[ E_4 \approx _{\sqrt{\nu _{\mathrm{evaluation}}}+\sqrt{\zeta }} \langle \psi \rvert G \otimes G \lvert \psi \rangle . \]

Thus the second quartic term differs from \(\langle \psi \rvert G \otimes G \lvert \psi \rangle \) by at most

\[ 2\sqrt{\zeta }+2\frac{dm}{q}+\sqrt{\nu _{\mathrm{evaluation}}}+\sqrt{\zeta } \le 6\sqrt{\zeta }+2m(d/q)+14m\left(\gamma ^{1/4}+\zeta ^{1/4}\right). \]

Combining this with the first-term estimate from Lemma 3.19 gives

\[ 12\zeta ^{1/2}+2m(d/q)+14m\left(\gamma ^{1/4}+\zeta ^{1/4}\right) \le 30m\left(\gamma ^{1/4}+\zeta ^{1/4}+(d/q)^{1/4}\right), \]

which is the claimed commutation bound.
