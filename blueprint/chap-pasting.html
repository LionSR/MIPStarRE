Theorem 9.1 Pasting
#

Uses

Let \((\psi ,A,B,L)\) be an \((\varepsilon ,\delta ,\gamma )\)-good symmetric strategy for the \((m+1,q,d)\) low individual degree test. Let \(\{ G^x\} _{x \in \mathbb {F}_q}\) be projective submeasurements in \(\mathrm{PolySub}(m,q,d)\) such that:

  1. if \(G = \mathbb {E}_x \sum _g G_g^x\), then \(\langle \psi \rvert G \otimes I \lvert \psi \rangle \ge 1-\kappa \);

  2. on average over \((u,x) \sim \mathbb {F}_q^{m+1}\),

    \[ A_a^{u,x} \otimes I \simeq _\zeta I \otimes G_{[g(u)=a]}^x; \]

  3. on average over \(x \sim \mathbb {F}_q\), one has \(G_g^x \otimes I \approx _\zeta I \otimes G_g^x\);

  4. there are positive semidefinite operators \(Z^x\) such that

    \[ \mathbb {E}_x \langle \psi \rvert (I-G^x) \otimes Z^x \lvert \psi \rangle \le \zeta , \qquad Z^x \ge \mathbb {E}_u A_{g(u)}^{u,x}. \]

Let \(k \ge 400md\) and set

\[ \nu = 100k^2m\left(\varepsilon ^{1/32}+\delta ^{1/32}+\gamma ^{1/32}+\zeta ^{1/32}+(d/q)^{1/32}\right) \]

and

\[ \sigma = \kappa \left(1+\frac{1}{100m}\right) + 2\nu + e^{-k/(80000m^2)}. \]

Then there exists a measurement \(H \in \mathrm{PolyMeas}(m+1,q,d)\) such that, on average over \(u \sim \mathbb {F}_q^{m+1}\),

\[ A_a^u \otimes I \simeq _\sigma I \otimes H_{[h(u)=a]}. \]
Proof ▶

First build a pasted submeasurement with the same consistency bound and with completeness at least \(1-\kappa (1+1/(100m)) - \nu - e^{-k/(80000m^2)}\); this is Lemma 9.3. Then add the missing mass to one distinguished polynomial outcome.

Lemma 9.2 Slice measurements are consistent with line answers

Under the hypotheses of Theorem 9.1, there exists a submeasurement \(H \in \mathrm{PolySub}(m+1,q,d)\) such that:

  1. on average over \(u \sim \mathbb {F}_q^{m+1}\),

    \[ A_a^u \otimes I \simeq _\nu I \otimes H_{[h(u)=a]}; \]

Definition 9.4 Distinct tuples
#

For \(k \ge 1\), let \(\mathsf{Distinct}_k \subseteq \mathbb {F}_q^k\) be the set of tuples \((x_1,\dots ,x_k)\) with pairwise distinct coordinates.

Lemma 9.5 Distinct tuples versus independent tuples
#

Uses

  • Definition 9.4

Lean declarations

  • MIPStarRE.LDT.Pasting.ldDnoteq

If \(x=(x_1,\dots ,x_k)\) is uniformly random in \(\mathbb {F}_q^k\) and \(y=(y_1,\dots ,y_k)\) is uniformly random in \(\mathsf{Distinct}_k\), then

\[ d_{\mathrm{TV}}(x,y) \le \frac{k^2}{q}. \]
Proof ▶

A uniformly random tuple fails to be distinct only if some pair of coordinates collides. Bound that collision probability by a union bound.

Lemma 9.6 A scalar inequality for the first construction

9.2 The completed slice measurements

Definition 9.7 The incomplete part and the completed measurement
#

Uses

  • Definition 3.4

For each \(x \in \mathbb {F}_q\), write \(G^x = \sum _g G_g^x\) and define the incomplete outcome \(G_\bot ^x = I-G^x\). The completed measurement \(\widehat G^x\) has outcome set \(\mathcal{P}(m,q,d) \cup \{ \bot \} \) and is given by

\[ \widehat G_g^x = G_g^x \quad (g \in \mathcal{P}(m,q,d)), \qquad \widehat G_\bot ^x = G_\bot ^x. \]
Definition 9.8 Types
#

A type is a vector \(\tau \in \{ 0,1\} ^k\). Its weight \(|\tau |\) records how many of the \(k\) outcomes belong to \(\mathcal{P}(m,q,d)\) rather than to the extra outcome \(\bot \).

Definition 9.9 The pasted measurement
#

Uses

  • Definition 9.7
  • Definition 9.8
  • Definition 9.4

Fix \(k \ge d+1\). For \(x_1,\dots ,x_k \in \mathbb {F}_q\) and outcomes \(g_1,\dots ,g_k \in \mathcal{P}(m,q,d) \cup \{ \bot \} \), define the sandwiched operator

\[ \widehat H_{g_1,\dots ,g_k}^{x_1,\dots ,x_k} = \widehat G_{g_1}^{x_1} \widehat G_{g_2}^{x_2} \cdots \widehat G_{g_k}^{x_k} \cdots \widehat G_{g_2}^{x_2} \widehat G_{g_1}^{x_1}. \]

If \((x_1,\dots ,x_k) \in \mathsf{Distinct}_k\) and \(h \in \mathcal{P}(m+1,q,d)\), define

\[ H_h^{x_1,\dots ,x_k} = \sum _{\tau \, :\, |\tau | \ge d+1} \widehat H_{h_\tau }^{x_1,\dots ,x_k}, \]

where \(h_\tau \) is the tuple whose \(i\)-th entry is \(h|_{x_i}\) when \(\tau _i=1\) and \(\bot \) when \(\tau _i=0\). The pasted submeasurement is the average

\[ H_h = \mathbb {E}_{(x_1,\dots ,x_k) \sim \mathsf{Distinct}_k} H_h^{x_1,\dots ,x_k}. \]

Lemma 9.12 Commutativity with \(G_g^x\) implies commutativity with \(G^x\)

\[ M_o^y \otimes I \approx _\omega I \otimes M_o^y \qquad \text{and}\qquad G_g^x M_o^y \otimes I \approx _\chi M_o^y G_g^x \otimes I \]

9.4 Completeness of the pasted measurement

Definition 9.19 Outcomes of a fixed type
#

Uses

  • Definition 9.8

For a type \(\tau \in \{ 0,1\} ^k\), let \(\mathsf{Outcomes}_\tau \) be the set of tuples \((g_1,\dots ,g_k)\) such that \(g_i \in \mathcal{P}(m,q,d)\) when \(\tau _i=1\) and \(g_i=\bot \) when \(\tau _i=0\). If \(x_1,\dots ,x_k \in \mathbb {F}_q\), let \(\mathsf{Global}_\tau (x)\) be the subset of \(\mathsf{Outcomes}_\tau \) that arises from restrictions of a single polynomial in \(\mathcal{P}(m+1,q,d)\).

Lemma 9.20 Summing over all outcomes
#

Uses

  • Definition 9.19
  • Lemma 9.5
  • Lemma 9.17
  • Lemma 3.2

Lean declarations

  • MIPStarRE.LDT.Pasting.overAllOutcomes

If \(x_1,\dots ,x_k\) are sampled independently and uniformly from \(\mathbb {F}_q\), then

\[ \langle \psi \rvert H \otimes I \lvert \psi \rangle \approx _{\nu _7} \mathbb {E}_{x_1,\dots ,x_k} \sum _{\tau \, :\, |\tau | \ge d+1} \sum _{(g_1,\dots ,g_k) \in \mathsf{Outcomes}_\tau } \langle \psi \rvert \widehat H_{g_1,\dots ,g_k}^{x_1,\dots ,x_k} \otimes I \lvert \psi \rangle , \]

where

\[ \nu _7 = 46k^2m(\varepsilon ^{1/32}+\delta ^{1/32}+\gamma ^{1/32}+\zeta ^{1/32}+(d/q)^{1/32}). \]
Proof ▶

The definition of \(H\) sums over tuples \((g_1,\dots ,g_k)\) that arise as restrictions of a single polynomial in \(\mathcal{P}(m+1,q,d)\). To bound the contribution of the remaining tuples, fix any type \(\tau \) with \(|\tau |\ge d+1\) and any tuple \((g_1,\dots ,g_k) \in \mathsf{Outcomes}_\tau \) that is not globally consistent, meaning no \(h \in \mathcal{P}(m+1,q,d)\) restricts to \((g_1,\dots ,g_k)\) on the active coordinates. Among the \(|\tau |\ge d+1\) active coordinates, at most \(d\) can agree with the unique interpolant \(h^*\) determined by any \(d+1\) of them (by the Schwartz–Zippel bound, Lemma 3.2), so there exists an index \(i^*\) with \(\tau _{i^*}=1\) and \(g_{i^*} \ne h^*|_{x_{i^*}}\). At that index, the linewise consistency from Lemma 9.17 shows the sandwiched contribution is at most \(\nu _5\), so the total globally-inconsistent mass is \(O(k\nu _5)\). Finally, replace the distinct-tuple distribution by independent tuples using Lemma 9.5, paying the TV cost \(k^2/q\).

Definition 9.21 Truncated type sums
#

Uses

  • Definition 9.8

For \(1 \le \ell \le k+1\) and a tail type \(\tau _{\ge \ell } \in \{ 0,1\} ^{k-\ell +1}\), define

\[ S_{\tau _{\ge \ell }} = \sum _{\tau _{{\lt}\ell }\, :\, |\tau | \ge d+1} G^{|\tau _{{\lt}\ell }|}(I-G)^{(\ell -1)-|\tau _{{\lt}\ell }|}. \]

This records the contribution of all prefixes that can still be completed to a type of weight at least \(d+1\) once the tail \(\tau _{\ge \ell }\) is fixed.

Lemma 9.22 Recurrence for truncated type sums
#

Uses

  • Definition 9.21

Each \(S_{\tau _{\ge \ell }}\) is Hermitian, positive semidefinite, and bounded by \(I\). Moreover, if \(\tau _{{\gt}\ell }\) is a tail type of length \(k-\ell \), then

\[ S_{\tau _{{\gt}\ell }} = \sum _{\tau _\ell \in \{ 0,1\} } S_{\tau _{\ge \ell }} G^{\tau _\ell }(I-G)^{1-\tau _\ell }. \]
Proof ▶

Every summand in \(S_{\tau _{\ge \ell }}\) is a polynomial in the commuting positive operators \(G\) and \(I-G\), so \(S_{\tau _{\ge \ell }}\) is Hermitian and positive. Summing over all prefixes gives

\[ S_{\tau _{\ge \ell }} \le (G+(I-G))^{\ell -1}=I. \]

The recurrence is obtained by splitting the next type bit according to whether the \(\ell \)-th coordinate contributes a genuine slice polynomial or the extra outcome \(\bot \).

Lemma 9.23 From the pasted sum to a polynomial in \(G\)
#

Uses

  • Lemma 9.15
  • Lemma 9.16
  • Definition 9.9
  • Definition 9.21
  • Lemma 9.22

Lean declarations

  • MIPStarRE.LDT.Pasting.fromHToG

If \(x_1,\dots ,x_k\) are sampled independently and uniformly from \(\mathbb {F}_q\), then

\[ \mathbb {E}_{x_1,\dots ,x_k} \sum _{\tau \, :\, |\tau | \ge d+1} \sum _{(g_1,\dots ,g_k) \in \mathsf{Outcomes}_\tau } \langle \psi \rvert \widehat H_{g_1,\dots ,g_k}^{x_1,\dots ,x_k} \otimes I \lvert \psi \rangle \approx _{\nu _8} \sum _{r=d+1}^k \binom {k}{r} \langle \psi \rvert G^r (I-G)^{k-r} \otimes I \lvert \psi \rangle , \]

where

\[ \nu _8 = 46km(\gamma ^{1/32}+\zeta ^{1/32}+(d/q)^{1/32}). \]
Proof ▶

Rewrite the unrestricted sandwiched sum using the matrices \(S_{\tau _{\ge \ell }}\) from Definition 9.21. For each stage \(\ell \), move the rightmost factor \(\widehat G_{g_\ell }^{x_\ell }\) from the left half of the sandwich to the second tensor factor, commute it past the remaining half-sandwich by Lemma 9.16, and then average over \(x_\ell \). Lemma 9.22 turns that average into multiplication by either \(G\) or \(I-G\), so one passes from \(S_{\tau _{\ge \ell }}\) to \(S_{\tau _{{\gt}\ell }}\). Iterating this recurrence for \(\ell =1,\dots ,k\) collapses the sandwiched expression to the Bernoulli polynomial \(\sum _{r=d+1}^k \binom {k}{r} G^r(I-G)^{k-r}\).

Lemma 9.24 Matrix Chernoff bound for the Bernoulli polynomial
#

Lean declarations

  • MIPStarRE.LDT.Pasting.chernoffBernoulliMatrix

Let \(0{\lt}\theta {\lt}1\) and integers \(k,d{\gt}0\) with \(k \ge 2d/\theta \). Define

\[ F(X) = \sum _{r=d+1}^k \binom {k}{r} X^r (I-X)^{k-r}. \]

If \(X\) is Hermitian, \(0 \le X \le I\), and \(\langle \psi \rvert X \otimes I \lvert \psi \rangle \ge 1-\kappa \), then

\[ \langle \psi \rvert F(X) \otimes I \lvert \psi \rangle \ge 1 - \frac{\kappa }{1-\theta } - e^{-\theta ^2 k/2}. \]
Proof ▶

Diagonalize \(X\), interpret the spectral measure of \(X\) against the reduced state of \(\lvert \psi \rangle \) as a probability distribution on eigenvalues, and apply the scalar Chernoff bound to that distribution.
