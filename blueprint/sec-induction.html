Let \((\psi ,A,B,L)\) be an \((\varepsilon ,\delta ,\gamma )\)-good symmetric strategy for the \((m,q,d)\) low individual degree test. Let \(G \in \mathrm{PolySub}(m,q,d)\) be a submeasurement with the following property:

  1. (Consistency with \(A\)): On average over \(u \sim \mathbb {F}_q^m\),

    \[ A_a^u \otimes I \simeq _\nu I \otimes G_{[g(u)=a]}. \]

Let

\[ \zeta = 3000m\left(\varepsilon ^{1/32}+\delta ^{1/32}+(d/q)^{1/32}\right). \]

Then there exists a projective submeasurement \(H \in \mathrm{PolySub}(m,q,d)\) with the following properties:

  1. (Completeness): If \(H=\sum _h H_h\), then

    \[ \langle \psi \rvert H \otimes I \lvert \psi \rangle \ge (1-\nu )-\zeta . \]

  2. (Consistency with \(A\)): On average over \(u \sim \mathbb {F}_q^m\),

    \[ A_a^u \otimes I \simeq _\zeta I \otimes H_{[h(u)=a]}. \]

  3. (Strong self-consistency):

    \[ H_h \otimes I \approx _\zeta I \otimes H_h. \]

  4. (Boundedness): There exists a positive semidefinite operator \(Z\) such that

    \[ \langle \psi \rvert Z \otimes (I-H) \lvert \psi \rangle \le \zeta \]

    and for each \(h \in \mathcal{P}(m,q,d)\),

    \[ Z \ge \mathbb {E}_u A_{h(u)}^u. \]
Proof ▶

Complete \(G\) to a measurement by adjoining an extra outcome \(\bot \). Apply Theorem 7.4 to the completed measurement, then discard the \(\bot \) outcome. By Lemma 3.40, the completeness, consistency, strong self-consistency, and boundedness estimates transfer back to the original polynomial outcomes with an additive error from the missing mass \(\langle \psi \rvert (I - \sum _g G_g) \otimes I \lvert \psi \rangle \). Since the output is projective, Lemma 3.34 converts the strong self-consistency statement to the usual bipartite formulation.

Let \((\psi ,A,B,L)\) be an \((\varepsilon ,\delta ,\gamma )\)-good symmetric strategy for the \((m,q,d)\) low individual degree test. Let \(k \ge md\) and set

\[ \nu = 1000k^2m^2\left(\varepsilon ^{1/1024}+\delta ^{1/1024}+\gamma ^{1/1024}+(d/q)^{1/1024}\right) \]

and

\[ \sigma = m^2\left(\nu + e^{-k/(80000m^2)}\right). \]

Then there exists a measurement \(G \in \mathrm{PolyMeas}(m,q,d)\) such that, on average over \(u \sim \mathbb {F}_q^m\),

\[ A_a^u \otimes I \simeq _\sigma I \otimes G_{[g(u)=a]}. \]
Proof ▶

In Lean, the base case and the internal successor-step assembly are now formalized through together with the bookkeeping packages that mirror the four paper stages \(\text{restrict} \to \text{induct} \to \text{self-improve} \to \text{paste}\). The remaining gap, tracked by issue #630, is a public wrapper that supplies the weighted restricted-probability inputs and the restricted-strategy self-improvement producer needed to invoke the successor theorem directly, so the displayed theorem is not yet marked . We may assume \(\varepsilon ,\delta ,\gamma ,d/q \le 1\), since otherwise \(\nu \ge 1\) and the bound is trivial.

The proof is by induction on \(m\). The base case \(m=1\) is Lemma 10.6.

For the step from \(m\) to \(m+1\), let \((\psi ,A,B,L)\) be an \((\varepsilon ,\delta ,\gamma )\)-good symmetric strategy for the \((m+1,q,d)\) test, and let \(k \ge (m+1)d\). For each \(x \in \mathbb {F}_q\), let \((\psi ,A^x,B^x,L^x)\) be the \(x\)-restricted strategy, with failure parameters \(\varepsilon _x,\delta _x,\gamma _x\). Apply the induction hypothesis to each restricted strategy with the same integer \(k\) and define

\[ \nu _x = 1000k^2m^2\left(\varepsilon _x^{1/1024}+\delta _x^{1/1024}+\gamma _x^{1/1024}+(d/q)^{1/1024}\right), \qquad \sigma _x = m^2\left(\nu _x + e^{-k/(80000m^2)}\right). \]

This yields a measurement \(G^x \in \mathrm{PolyMeas}(m,q,d)\) such that

\[ (A^x)_a^u \otimes I \simeq _{\sigma _x} I \otimes G_{[g(u)=a]}^x. \]

Next define

\[ \zeta _x = 3000m\left(\varepsilon _x^{1/32}+\delta _x^{1/32}+(d/q)^{1/32}\right). \]

Theorem 10.4 produces a projective submeasurement \(\widehat G^x \in \mathrm{PolySub}(m,q,d)\) such that, for each \(x\):

  1. if \(\widehat G^x=\sum _g \widehat G_g^x\), then

    \[ \langle \psi \rvert \widehat G^x \otimes I \lvert \psi \rangle \ge (1-\sigma _x)-\zeta _x; \]

  2. on average over \(u \sim \mathbb {F}_q^m\),

    \[ (A^x)_a^u \otimes I \simeq _{\zeta _x} I \otimes \widehat G_{[g(u)=a]}^x; \]
  3. \[ \widehat G_g^x \otimes I \approx _{\zeta _x} I \otimes \widehat G_g^x; \]

  4. there exists a positive semidefinite operator \(Z^x\) such that

    \[ \langle \psi \rvert Z^x \otimes (I-\widehat G^x) \lvert \psi \rangle \le \zeta _x \]

    and for each \(g \in \mathcal{P}(m,q,d)\),

    \[ Z^x \ge \mathbb {E}_u (A^x)_{g(u)}^u. \]

We now average the slice-dependent parameters. By concavity of \(\alpha \mapsto \alpha ^c\) for \(c \le 1\) and Lemma 10.3,

\begin{align*} \mathbb {E}_x \nu _x & \le 1000k^2m^2\left((\mathbb {E}_x \varepsilon _x)^{1/1024}+(\mathbb {E}_x \delta _x)^{1/1024}+(\mathbb {E}_x \gamma _x)^{1/1024}+(d/q)^{1/1024}\right) \\ & \le 1000k^2m^2\left(\left(\frac{m+1}{m}\varepsilon \right)^{1/1024}+\delta ^{1/1024}+\left(\frac{m+1}{m}\gamma \right)^{1/1024}+(d/q)^{1/1024}\right) \\ & \le 1000k^2(m+1)^2\left(\varepsilon ^{1/1024}+\delta ^{1/1024}+\gamma ^{1/1024}+(d/q)^{1/1024}\right). \end{align*}

Call this final quantity \(\nu \). Then, defining

\[ \sigma = m^2\left(\nu + e^{-k/(80000m^2)}\right), \]

we have

\[ \sigma \ge \mathbb {E}_x \sigma _x. \]

Similarly,

\begin{align*} \mathbb {E}_x \zeta _x & \le 3000m\left((\mathbb {E}_x \varepsilon _x)^{1/32}+(\mathbb {E}_x \delta _x)^{1/32}+(d/q)^{1/32}\right) \\ & \le 3000m\left(\left(\frac{m+1}{m}\varepsilon \right)^{1/32}+\delta ^{1/32}+(d/q)^{1/32}\right) \\ & \le 3000(m+1)\left(\varepsilon ^{1/32}+\delta ^{1/32}+(d/q)^{1/32}\right). \end{align*}

Call this last quantity \(\zeta \). For later use,

\begin{align} \zeta & = 3000(m+1)\left(\varepsilon ^{1/32}+\delta ^{1/32}+(d/q)^{1/32}\right) \notag \\ & \le 1000k^2(m+1)^2\left(\varepsilon ^{1/32}+\delta ^{1/32}+(d/q)^{1/32}\right) \notag \\ & \le 1000k^2(m+1)^2\left(\varepsilon ^{1/1024}+\delta ^{1/1024}+\gamma ^{1/1024}+(d/q)^{1/1024}\right) \notag \\ & = \nu . \label{eq:zeta-smaller-than-nu} \end{align}

The averaged slice family \(\{ \widehat G^x\} _{x \in \mathbb {F}_q}\) therefore satisfies the four hypotheses of Theorem 10.5 with completeness parameter \(\kappa =\sigma +\zeta \). To verify the new \(\nu \) bound in that theorem, observe that \((3000)^{1/32} \le 2\), so

\[ \zeta ^{1/32} \le 2(m+1)\left(\varepsilon ^{1/1024}+\delta ^{1/1024}+(d/q)^{1/1024}\right), \]

and hence

\begin{align*} & 100k^2m\left(\varepsilon ^{1/32}+\delta ^{1/32}+\gamma ^{1/32}+\zeta ^{1/32}+(d/q)^{1/32}\right) \\ & \le 1000k^2(m+1)^2\left(\varepsilon ^{1/1024}+\delta ^{1/1024}+\gamma ^{1/1024}+(d/q)^{1/1024}\right) \\ & = \nu . \end{align*}

Therefore Theorem 10.5 gives a pasted measurement \(H \in \mathrm{PolyMeas}(m+1,q,d)\) such that, on average over \(u \sim \mathbb {F}_q^{m+1}\),

\[ A_a^u \otimes I \simeq _{\sigma ^*} I \otimes H_{[h(u)=a]}, \]

where

\[ \sigma ^* = (\sigma +\zeta )\left(1+\frac{1}{100m}\right) + 2\nu + e^{-k/(80000m^2)}. \]

It remains to compare \(\sigma ^*\) with the next-stage target:

\begin{align} \sigma ^* & = (\sigma +\zeta )\left(1+\frac{1}{100m}\right) + 2\nu + e^{-k/(80000m^2)} \notag \\ & \le (\sigma +\nu )\left(1+\frac{1}{100m}\right) + 2\nu + e^{-k/(80000m^2)} \tag {by \ref{eq:zeta-smaller-than-nu}} \notag \\ & = \left(1+\frac{1}{100m}\right)\left(m^2\left(\nu +e^{-k/(80000m^2)}\right)+\nu \right) + 2\nu + e^{-k/(80000m^2)} \notag \\ & \le \left(1+\frac{1}{100m}\right)(m^2+3)\left(\nu +e^{-k/(80000m^2)}\right). \label{eq:gonna-bound-m-function} \end{align}

Since \(m \ge 2\),

\[ \left(1+\frac{1}{100m}\right)(m^2+3) \le (m+1)^2. \]

Applying this to 2 yields

\[ \sigma ^* \le (m+1)^2\left(\nu +e^{-k/(80000m^2)}\right) \le (m+1)^2\left(\nu +e^{-k/(80000(m+1)^2)}\right), \]

which is exactly the bound required for dimension \(m+1\).

Let \((\psi ,A^{\mathrm A},B^{\mathrm A},L^{\mathrm A},A^{\mathrm B},B^{\mathrm B},L^{\mathrm B})\) be a projective strategy passing the \((m,q,d)\) low individual degree test with probability at least \(1-\varepsilon \). Now unsymmetrize by defining

\[ G_g^{\mathrm A} = (\langle 0 \rvert \otimes I)\, G_g\, (\lvert 0 \rangle \otimes I), \qquad G_g^{\mathrm B} = (\langle 1 \rvert \otimes I)\, G_g\, (\lvert 1 \rangle \otimes I). \]

These are POVMs on the original prover space. Since \(A_{\mathrm{sym}}\) is a convex combination of \((\lvert 0 \rangle \langle 0 \rvert \otimes I)\) and \((\lvert 1 \rangle \langle 1 \rvert \otimes I)\) weighted by the test probabilities, the cross-terms \(\langle 0 \rvert \cdot A_{\mathrm{sym}} \cdot \lvert 1 \rangle \) vanish. Expanding the consistency of \(G\) gives

\begin{gather} G_{[g(u)=a]}^{\mathrm A} \otimes I \simeq _{2\sigma } I \otimes A_a^{\mathrm B,u}, \label{eq:cons-a}\\ I \otimes G_{[g(u)=a]}^{\mathrm B} \simeq _{2\sigma } A_a^{\mathrm A,u} \otimes I. \label{eq:cons-b} \end{gather}

Since the original strategy is \((3\varepsilon ,3\varepsilon ,3\varepsilon )\)-good,

\[ A_a^{\mathrm A,u} \otimes I \simeq _{3\varepsilon } I \otimes A_a^{\mathrm B,u}. \]

Therefore Lemma 3.27 yields

\[ G_{[g(u)=a]}^{\mathrm A} \otimes I \simeq _{2\sigma + 2\sqrt{3\varepsilon +2\sigma }} I \otimes G_{[g(u)=a]}^{\mathrm B}. \]

Expanding this consistency relation and applying Lemma 3.7 to replace point evaluations by polynomial evaluations (at cost \(md/q\)) gives

\begin{equation} \label{eq:G-self-consistency} G_g^{\mathrm A} \otimes I \simeq _{\zeta _1} I \otimes G_g^{\mathrm B}, \end{equation}
6

where

\[ \zeta _1 = 2\sigma + 2\sqrt{3\varepsilon +2\sigma } + \frac{md}{q}. \]

Apply Lemma 4.5 to 6. This gives projective submeasurements \(P^{\mathrm A},P^{\mathrm B} \in \mathrm{PolySub}(m,q,d)\) with

\[ G_g^{\mathrm A} \otimes I \approx _{100\zeta _1^{1/4}} P_g^{\mathrm A} \otimes I, \qquad I \otimes G_g^{\mathrm B} \approx _{100\zeta _1^{1/4}} I \otimes P_g^{\mathrm B}. \]

Completing them by Lemma 3.40 yields projective measurements \(Q^{\mathrm A},Q^{\mathrm B} \in \mathrm{PolyMeas}(m,q,d)\) such that

\begin{align} G_g^{\mathrm A} \otimes I & \approx _{\zeta _2} Q_g^{\mathrm A} \otimes I, \label{eq:G-with-Q-A} \\ I \otimes G_g^{\mathrm B} & \approx _{\zeta _2} I \otimes Q_g^{\mathrm B}, \notag \end{align}

where

\[ \zeta _2 = 200\zeta _1^{1/4} + 40\zeta _1^{1/8}. \]

By 6 and Lemma 3.20,

\[ G_g^{\mathrm A} \otimes I \approx _{2\zeta _1} I \otimes G_g^{\mathrm B}. \]

Hence Lemma 3.26 gives

\[ Q_g^{\mathrm A} \otimes I \approx _{\zeta _3} I \otimes Q_g^{\mathrm B}, \]

where

\[ \zeta _3 = 6\zeta _1 + 6\zeta _2. \]

Because both measurements are projective, Lemma 3.20 converts this to

\begin{equation} \label{eq:third-goal} Q_g^{\mathrm A} \otimes I \simeq _{\zeta _3/2} I \otimes Q_g^{\mathrm B}. \end{equation}
8

By data processing,

\begin{equation} \label{eq:just-data-processed-the-heck-outta-this} Q_{[g(u)=a]}^{\mathrm A} \otimes I \simeq _{\zeta _3/2} I \otimes Q_{[g(u)=a]}^{\mathrm B}. \end{equation}
9

Next, Lemma 3.19, applied to 6 and 7, implies

\[ Q_g^{\mathrm A} \otimes I \simeq _{\zeta _1} I \otimes G_g^{\mathrm B}. \]

Data processing then gives

\begin{equation} \label{eq:ok-almost-there-ok} Q_{[g(u)=a]}^{\mathrm A} \otimes I \simeq _{\zeta _1} I \otimes G_{[g(u)=a]}^{\mathrm B}. \end{equation}

Applying Lemma 3.27 to 5, 10, and 9 yields

\begin{equation} \label{eq:one-goal} A_a^{\mathrm A,u} \otimes I \simeq _{\zeta _4} I \otimes Q_{[g(u)=a]}^{\mathrm B}, \end{equation}

where

\[ \zeta _4 = 2\sigma + 2\sqrt{\zeta _1+\zeta _3/2}. \]

The same argument with the roles reversed gives

\begin{equation} \label{eq:another-goal} I \otimes A_a^{\mathrm B,u} \simeq _{\zeta _4} Q_{[g(u)=a]}^{\mathrm A} \otimes I. \end{equation}

With \(E\) as in Definition 10.9, the quantities \(\sigma ,\zeta _1,\zeta _2,\zeta _3,\zeta _4\) form the cascade of Definition 10.11. Theorem 10.12 and Theorem 10.13 package the final inflation to the scale \(100000k^2m^4 E\). The underlying paper estimates are:

\[ \sigma \le 10000k^2m^4\left(\varepsilon ^{1/1024}+(d/q)^{1/1024}+e^{-k/(80000m^2)}\right). \]
