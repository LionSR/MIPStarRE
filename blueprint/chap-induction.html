10 The main induction step

10.1 Restricting to one slice

Definition 10.1 Appending a line at height \(x\)
#

For \(x \in \mathbb {F}_q\), let \(\operatorname {append}_x(\ell )\) be the line in \(\mathbb {F}_q^{m+1}\) obtained by appending the coordinate \(x\) to every point of a line \(\ell \subseteq \mathbb {F}_q^m\). If \(f\colon \ell \to \mathbb {F}_q\), let \(\operatorname {append}_x(f)\) be the polynomial on \(\operatorname {append}_x(\ell )\) defined by \(\operatorname {append}_x(f)(u,x)=f(u)\).

Definition 10.2 \(x\)-restricted strategy
#

Uses

Let \((\psi ,A,B,L)\) be a symmetric strategy for the \((m+1,q,d)\) low individual degree test. For each \(x \in \mathbb {F}_q\), the \(x\)-restricted strategy \((\psi ,A^x,B^x,L^x)\) for the \((m,q,d)\) test is defined by

\[ (A^x)_a^u = A_a^{u,x}, \qquad (B^x)_f^{\ell } = B_{\operatorname {append}_x(f)}^{\operatorname {append}_x(\ell )}, \qquad (L^x)_f^{\ell } = L_{\operatorname {append}_x(f)}^{\operatorname {append}_x(\ell )}. \]
Lemma 10.3 Restricted success probabilities

Let \((\psi ,A,B,L)\) be an \((\varepsilon ,\delta ,\gamma )\)-good symmetric strategy for the \((m+1,q,d)\) test. For each \(x \in \mathbb {F}_q\), let \((\psi ,A^x,B^x,L^x)\) be the restricted strategy and let \(\varepsilon _x,\delta _x,\gamma _x\) be its three failure probabilities. Then

\[ \mathbb {E}_x \varepsilon _x \le \frac{m+1}{m} \varepsilon , \qquad \mathbb {E}_x \delta _x \le \delta , \qquad \mathbb {E}_x \gamma _x \le \frac{m+1}{m} \gamma . \]
Proof ▶

The self-consistency test restricts exactly to the slice at height \(x\). For the axis-parallel and diagonal-line tests, conditioning on the sampled line not being parallel to the last coordinate identifies the test with the corresponding restricted game.

Let \(G \in \mathrm{PolySub}(m,q,d)\) be a submeasurement such that, on average over \(u \sim \mathbb {F}_q^m\),

\[ A_a^u \otimes I \simeq _\nu I \otimes G_{[g(u)=a]}. \]

Set

\[ \zeta = 3000m\left(\varepsilon ^{1/32}+\delta ^{1/32}+(d/q)^{1/32}\right). \]

Then there exists a projective submeasurement \(H \in \mathrm{PolySub}(m,q,d)\) such that:

  1. if \(H=\sum _h H_h\), then \(\langle \psi \rvert H \otimes I \lvert \psi \rangle \ge (1-\nu )-\zeta \);

  2. on average over \(u \sim \mathbb {F}_q^m\),

    \[ A_a^u \otimes I \simeq _\zeta I \otimes H_{[h(u)=a]}; \]

  3. \(H_h \otimes I \approx _\zeta I \otimes H_h\);

  4. there exists a positive semidefinite operator \(Z\) such that

    \[ \langle \psi \rvert Z \otimes (I-H) \lvert \psi \rangle \le \zeta , \]

    and \(Z \ge \mathbb {E}_u A_{h(u)}^u\) for every \(h \in \mathcal{P}(m,q,d)\).

Proof ▶

Complete the input submeasurement \(G\) by adjoining an extra outcome \(\bot \) for its missing mass. Apply Theorem 7.4 to this completed measurement. The output is still indexed by the enlarged answer set, so one then discards the \(\bot \) outcome and transfers the estimates back to the original polynomial outcomes. The quantitative work is exactly the completion bookkeeping from Lemma 3.29: the helper theorem supplies completeness, consistency, strong self-consistency, and boundedness for the completed family, and these pass to the original submeasurement after data processing and one final triangle-inequality step.

Let \((\psi ,A,B,L)\) be an \((\varepsilon ,\delta ,\gamma )\)-good symmetric strategy for the \((m,q,d)\) low individual degree test. Let \(k \ge md\) and set

\[ \nu = 1000k^2m^2\left(\varepsilon ^{1/1024}+\delta ^{1/1024}+\gamma ^{1/1024}+(d/q)^{1/1024}\right) \]

and

\[ \sigma = m^2\left(\nu + e^{-k/(80000m^2)}\right). \]

Then there exists a measurement \(G \in \mathrm{PolyMeas}(m,q,d)\) such that, on average over \(u \sim \mathbb {F}_q^m\),

\[ A_a^u \otimes I \simeq _\sigma I \otimes G_{[g(u)=a]}. \]
Proof ▶

Proceed by induction on \(m\). For \(m=1\), the unique axis-parallel line measurement already gives the required global measurement. For the step from \(m\) to \(m+1\), restrict the strategy to each slice \(x\) and apply the induction hypothesis with the same \(k\), producing slice measurements \(G^x\) with slice-dependent errors \(\nu _x\) and \(\sigma _x\). Next self-improve each \(G^x\) to a projective submeasurement \(\widehat G^x\) with a third slice-dependent parameter

\[ \zeta _x = 3000m\left(\varepsilon _x^{1/32}+\delta _x^{1/32}+(d/q)^{1/32}\right). \]

Lemma 10.3 and concavity of \(\alpha \mapsto \alpha ^c\) for \(c\le 1\) give averaged bounds \(\mathbb {E}_x\nu _x \le \nu \) and \(\mathbb {E}_x\sigma _x \le \sigma \). For the self-improvement term,

\[ \mathbb {E}_x \zeta _x \le 3000m\left(\left(\frac{m+1}{m}\varepsilon \right)^{1/32}+\delta ^{1/32}+(d/q)^{1/32}\right) \le 6000m\left(\varepsilon ^{1/32}+\delta ^{1/32}+(d/q)^{1/32}\right) =:\zeta , \]

and this \(\zeta \) is at most \(\nu \) because \(k\ge md\ge 1\) and \(\alpha ^{1/32}\le \alpha ^{1/1024}\) for \(0\le \alpha \le 1\). Theorem 9.1 then pastes the family \(\{ \widehat G^x\} \) into a global measurement with error

\[ \sigma ^* = (\sigma +\zeta )\left(1+\frac{1}{100m}\right) + 2\nu + e^{-k/(80000m^2)}. \]

The final arithmetic check is that \(\sigma ^* \le (m+1)^2\bigl(\nu +e^{-k/(80000(m+1)^2)}\bigr)\), which is exactly the next-stage value of \(\sigma \). This exposes the quantitative cascade hidden behind the one-line induction summary.

10.3 Proof of the main formal theorem

Proof ▶

Start from the original two-prover strategy and symmetrize it by adjoining a classical role register. The symmetrized strategy is still \((3\varepsilon ,3\varepsilon ,3\varepsilon )\)-good, so Theorem 10.5 gives a global measurement \(G\) on the enlarged space with consistency error \(\sigma \). Projecting \(G\) to the two role sectors yields POVMs \(G^{\mathrm A}\) and \(G^{\mathrm B}\) for the original provers. Because the symmetrized point measurement is block diagonal in the role register, the cross-terms vanish, and evaluating the induction estimate on the \((0,1)\) and \((1,0)\) sectors gives

\[ G^{\mathrm A}_{[g(u)=a]} \otimes I \simeq _{2\sigma } I \otimes A_a^{\mathrm B,u}, \qquad I \otimes G^{\mathrm B}_{[g(u)=a]} \simeq _{2\sigma } A_a^{\mathrm A,u} \otimes I. \]

Combining these with the original point-measurement consistency \(A_a^{\mathrm A,u} \otimes I \simeq _{3\varepsilon } I \otimes A_a^{\mathrm B,u}\) and Lemma 3.16 shows that the post-processed families \(G^{\mathrm A}_{[g(u)=a]}\) and \(G^{\mathrm B}_{[g(u)=a]}\) are mutually consistent with parameter \(2\sigma +2\sqrt{3\varepsilon +2\sigma }\). Expanding this inconsistency and then removing the indicator \(g(u)\ne h(u)\) by Lemma 3.2 introduces the first intermediate parameter

\[ \zeta _1 = 2\sigma + 2\sqrt{3\varepsilon +2\sigma } + \frac{md}{q}. \]

Apply Lemma 4.2 to obtain projective submeasurements \(P^{\mathrm A}\) and \(P^{\mathrm B}\), and then complete them to projective measurements \(Q^{\mathrm A}\) and \(Q^{\mathrm B}\) using Lemma 3.29. This introduces

\[ \zeta _2 = 200\zeta _1^{1/4} + 40\zeta _1^{1/8}. \]

Comparing \(Q^{\mathrm A}\) and \(Q^{\mathrm B}\) through the original \(G^{\mathrm A},G^{\mathrm B}\) gives a second self-consistency parameter

\[ \zeta _3 = 6\zeta _1 + 6\zeta _2, \]

and data processing plus one last triangle inequality with the original point measurements gives the final consistency error

\[ \zeta _4 = 2\sigma + 2\sqrt{\zeta _1 + \zeta _3/2}. \]

Since \(\varepsilon \), \(d/q\), and the exponential tail are at most \(1\), one may repeatedly use \(\alpha \le \alpha ^{1/40000}\) and \(m,k \ge 1\) to absorb every intermediate exponent into the final scale. In particular, \(\sigma \) is \(O(k^2m^4(\varepsilon ^{1/40000}+(d/q)^{1/40000}+e^{-k/(2560000m^2)}))\), the same is then true for \(\zeta _1\), and therefore also for \(\zeta _2\), \(\zeta _3\), and \(\zeta _4\). Enlarging the universal constant to \(100000\) gives

\[ 100000k^2m^4\left(\varepsilon ^{1/40000} + (d/q)^{1/40000} + e^{-k/(2560000m^2)}\right). \]

Thus both \(\zeta _3/2\) and \(\zeta _4\) are bounded by the displayed quantity. Renaming the final projective measurements \(Q^{\mathrm A},Q^{\mathrm B}\) as the theorem’s \(G^{\mathrm A},G^{\mathrm B}\) gives exactly the stated conclusion.
