3.1 Polynomials and measurements

Definition 3.1 Low individual degree polynomials
✓

Let \(\mathcal{P}(m,q,d)\) be the set of functions \(g\colon \mathbb {F}_q^m \to \mathbb {F}_q\) that are represented by polynomials whose degree in each variable is at most \(d\).

Lemma 3.2 Schwartz–Zippel for individual degree
#

Uses

  • Definition 3.1

If \(g,h \in \mathcal{P}(m,q,d)\) are distinct, then

\[ \mathbb {P}_{u \sim \mathbb {F}_q^m}[g(u)=h(u)] \le \frac{md}{q}. \]
Proof ▶

The difference \(g-h\) is a nonzero polynomial of total degree at most \(md\). Apply Schwartz–Zippel  [ .

Definition 3.3 Measurements and submeasurements
#

Let \(\mathcal H\) be a Hilbert space and let \(\mathcal A\) be a finite outcome set. A submeasurement on \(\mathcal A\) is a family \(A=\{ A_a\} _{a \in \mathcal A}\) of Hermitian positive semidefinite operators on \(\mathcal H\) such that \(\sum _a A_a \le I\). It is a measurement when \(\sum _a A_a = I\), and it is projective when each \(A_a\) is an idempotent projection.

3.2 Consistency and state-dependent distance

Definition 3.7 Consistency
#

Lean declarations

  • MIPStarRE.LDT.Preliminaries.consistency

Let \(\lvert \psi \rangle \in \mathcal H_{\mathrm A} \otimes \mathcal H_{\mathrm B}\). Let \(A=\{ A_a^x\} \) and \(B=\{ B_a^x\} \) be submeasurements with the same answer set, and let \(\mathcal D\) be a distribution on the question set. We write

\[ A_a^x \otimes I \simeq _\delta I \otimes B_a^x \]

when

\[ \mathbb {E}_{x \sim \mathcal D} \sum _{a \ne b} \langle \psi \rvert A_a^x \otimes B_b^x \lvert \psi \rangle \le \delta . \]
Lemma 3.8 Characterization of a good symmetric strategy
#

Uses

  • Definition 2.4
  • Definition 3.7
  • Lemma 3.9

A symmetric projective strategy \((\psi ,A,B,L)\) is \((\varepsilon ,\delta ,\gamma )\)-good if and only if the following hold on the relevant test distributions:

\[ A_a^u \otimes I \simeq _\varepsilon I \otimes B^{\ell }_{[f(u)=a]}, \qquad A_a^u \otimes I \simeq _\delta I \otimes A_a^u, \qquad A_a^u \otimes I \simeq _\gamma I \otimes L^{\ell }_{[f(u)=a]}. \]
Proof ▶

Each subtest accepts exactly when the two measurement outcomes agree. Because the relevant families are measurements, Lemma 3.9 rewrites those acceptance probabilities as consistency bounds.

Lemma 3.9 Consistency for measurements
#

Uses

  • Definition 3.7

Lean declarations

  • MIPStarRE.LDT.Preliminaries.simeqForMeasurements

If \(A=\{ A_a^x\} \) and \(B=\{ B_a^x\} \) are measurements, then

\[ A_a^x \otimes I \simeq _\delta I \otimes B_a^x \qquad \text{if and only if}\qquad \mathbb {E}_x \sum _a \langle \psi \rvert A_a^x \otimes B_a^x \lvert \psi \rangle \ge 1-\delta . \]
Proof ▶

Expand the off-diagonal sum by writing \(\sum _{a \ne b} A_a^x \otimes B_b^x = \sum _b (I-A_b^x) \otimes B_b^x\).

Definition 3.10 State-dependent distance
#

Lean declarations

  • MIPStarRE.LDT.Preliminaries.stateDependentDistance

Let \(\lvert \psi \rangle \in \mathcal H\), let \(A=\{ A_a^x\} \) and \(B=\{ B_a^x\} \) be families of operators on \(\mathcal H\), and let \(\mathcal D\) be a distribution on the question set. We write

\[ A_a^x \approx _\delta B_a^x \]

when

\[ \mathbb {E}_{x \sim \mathcal D} \sum _a \lVert (A_a^x-B_a^x)\lvert \psi \rangle \rVert ^2 \le \delta . \]
Lemma 3.11 Transferring consistency through state-dependent distance
#

Uses

  • Definition 3.7
  • Definition 3.10
  • Lemma 3.9

Let \(A=\{ A_a^x\} \) and \(B=\{ B_a^x\} \) be measurements and let \(C=\{ C_a^x\} \) be a submeasurement. If \(A_a^x \otimes I \simeq _\delta I \otimes C_a^x\) and \(A_a^x \otimes I \approx _\varepsilon B_a^x \otimes I\), then

\[ B_a^x \otimes I \simeq _{\delta +\sqrt\varepsilon } I \otimes C_a^x. \]
Proof ▶

Rewrite the inconsistency with \(C\) as total mass minus diagonal overlap. The total mass is unchanged because \(A\) and \(B\) are measurements, and the diagonal overlap changes by at most \(\sqrt\varepsilon \) by Cauchy–Schwarz.

Lemma 3.12 Consistency implies state-dependent distance for measurements
#

Uses

  • Definition 3.7
  • Definition 3.10
  • Lemma 3.9

Lean declarations

  • MIPStarRE.LDT.Preliminaries.simeqToApprox

If \(A\) and \(B\) are measurements and \(A_a^x \otimes I \simeq _\delta I \otimes B_a^x\), then

\[ A_a^x \otimes I \approx _{2\delta } I \otimes B_a^x. \]

If both measurements are projective, the converse also holds.

Proof ▶

Expand the squared norm of \((A_a^x \otimes I - I \otimes B_a^x)\lvert \psi \rangle \) and use the diagonal-overlap formula from Lemma 3.9.

Lemma 3.13 Closeness of inner products
#

Uses

  • Definition 3.10

Let \(\{ A_a^x\} \), \(\{ B_a^x\} \), and \(\{ C_{a,b}^x\} \) be operator families. If \(A_a^x \approx _\gamma B_a^x\) and \(\sum _a (\sum _b C_{a,b}^x)(\sum _b C_{a,b}^x)^\dagger \le I\) for every \(x\), then

\[ \mathbb {E}_x \sum _{a,b} \langle \psi \rvert C_{a,b}^x A_a^x \lvert \psi \rangle \approx _{\sqrt\gamma } \mathbb {E}_x \sum _{a,b} \langle \psi \rvert C_{a,b}^x B_a^x \lvert \psi \rangle . \]

The analogous statement also holds when the \(C_{a,b}^x\) act on the right.

Proof ▶

Apply Cauchy–Schwarz to the difference of the two expressions and use the normalization hypothesis on the \(C_{a,b}^x\).

Lemma 3.14 Triangle inequality for vectors squared
#

For vectors \(\lvert \psi _1 \rangle ,\dots ,\lvert \psi _k \rangle \),

\[ \bigl\lVert \lvert \psi _1 \rangle +\cdots +\lvert \psi _k \rangle \bigr\rVert ^2 \le k(\lVert \lvert \psi _1 \rangle \rVert ^2+\cdots +\lVert \lvert \psi _k \rangle \rVert ^2). \]
Proof ▶

Apply the scalar inequality \((x_1+\cdots +x_k)^2 \le k(x_1^2+\cdots +x_k^2)\) to the norms and combine it with the triangle inequality.

Lemma 3.15 Triangle inequality for \(\approx _\delta \)
#

Uses

  • Definition 3.10
  • Lemma 3.14

Suppose \((A_i)_a^x \approx _{\delta _i} (A_{i+1})_a^x\) for \(i=1,\dots ,k\). Then

\[ (A_1)_a^x \approx _{k(\delta _1+\cdots +\delta _k)} (A_{k+1})_a^x. \]
Proof ▶

Expand \((A_1-A_{k+1})\lvert \psi \rangle \) as a telescoping sum and apply Lemma 3.14.

Lemma 3.16 Triangle inequality for \(\simeq _\delta \)
#

Uses

  • Lemma 3.12
  • Lemma 3.15
  • Lemma 3.11

Suppose \(A_a^x \otimes I \simeq _\varepsilon I \otimes B_a^x\), \(C_a^x \otimes I \simeq _\delta I \otimes B_a^x\), and \(C_a^x \otimes I \simeq _\gamma I \otimes D_a^x\), where all four families are measurements. Then

\[ A_a^x \otimes I \simeq _{\varepsilon + 2\sqrt{\delta +\gamma }} I \otimes D_a^x. \]
Proof ▶

Convert the two consistencies involving \(C\) to state-dependent distance, compose them by the triangle inequality, and transfer the result back to consistency by Lemma 3.11.

Lemma 3.17 Data processing for consistency
#

Uses

  • Definition 3.7

If \(A=\{ A_a^x\} \) and \(B=\{ B_a^x\} \) are measurements such that \(A_a^x \otimes I \simeq _\delta I \otimes B_a^x\), and \(f\) is a map on the answer set, then

Lemma 3.18 Consistency controls a submeasurement
#

Uses

  • Definition 3.7
  • Definition 3.10

Lean declarations

  • MIPStarRE.LDT.Preliminaries.consSubMeas

Let \(A=\{ A_a^x\} \) be a submeasurement and \(B=\{ B_a^x\} \) a measurement such that \(A_a^x \otimes I \simeq _\gamma I \otimes B_a^x\). Then

\[ A_a^x \otimes I \approx _\gamma A_a^x \otimes B_a^x \approx _\gamma A^x \otimes B_a^x, \]

where \(A^x=\sum _a A_a^x\). In particular,

\[ A_a^x \otimes I \approx _{4\gamma } A^x \otimes B_a^x. \]
Proof ▶

The inconsistency bound controls the mass of \(A_a^x(I-B_a^x)\) and of \((A^x-A_a^x)B_a^x\). Combine the two estimates with the triangle inequality for \(\approx _\delta \).

Lemma 3.19 Switch-sandwich
#

Uses

  • Definition 3.10

Lean declarations

  • MIPStarRE.LDT.Preliminaries.switchSandwich

Suppose \(A=\{ A_a^x\} \) is a projective submeasurement satisfying \(A_a^x \otimes I \approx _\delta I \otimes A_a^x\). Then for every operator \(B\) with \(0 \le B \le I\),

\[ \mathbb {E}_x \sum _a \langle \psi \rvert A_a^x B A_a^x \otimes I \lvert \psi \rangle \approx _{2\sqrt\delta } \mathbb {E}_x \sum _a \langle \psi \rvert B \otimes A_a^x \lvert \psi \rangle \approx _{\sqrt\delta } \mathbb {E}_x \sum _a \langle \psi \rvert BA_a^x \otimes I \lvert \psi \rangle . \]
Proof ▶

Move one copy of \(A_a^x\) across the bipartition using the \(\approx _\delta \) hypothesis, then use projectivity to collapse the resulting sandwich.

3.3 Strong self-consistency

Lemma 3.20 Completeness transfer to a projective approximation
#

Uses

  • Definition 3.10

Lean declarations

  • MIPStarRE.LDT.Preliminaries.completenessTransferProjectiveP

Let \(A=\{ A_a^x\} \) be a submeasurement and \(P=\{ P_a^x\} \) a projective submeasurement such that \(A_a^x \otimes I \approx _\varepsilon P_a^x \otimes I\). Then

\[ \langle \psi \rvert A \otimes I \lvert \psi \rangle \ge \langle \psi \rvert P \otimes I \lvert \psi \rangle - 2\sqrt\varepsilon . \]
Proof ▶

Compare the completeness of \(P\) with the diagonal overlaps \(\sum _a \langle \psi \rvert A_a^x P_a^x \otimes I \lvert \psi \rangle \) and \(\sum _a \langle \psi \rvert (A_a^x)^2 \otimes I \lvert \psi \rangle \).

Definition 3.21 Strong self-consistency
#

Lean declarations

  • MIPStarRE.LDT.Preliminaries.strongSelfConsistency

Let \(\lvert \psi \rangle \) be permutation-invariant and let \(A=\{ A_a^x\} \) be a submeasurement. We say that \(A\) is \(\delta \)-strongly self-consistent when

\[ \mathbb {E}_x \sum _a \langle \psi \rvert A_a^x \otimes A_a^x \lvert \psi \rangle \ge \langle \psi \rvert A \otimes I \lvert \psi \rangle - \delta . \]
Lemma 3.22 Strong self-consistency implies ordinary self-consistency
#

Uses

  • Definition 3.21
  • Definition 3.7

If \(A\) is \(\delta \)-strongly self-consistent, then \(A_a^x \otimes I \simeq _\delta I \otimes A_a^x\). If \(A\) is a measurement, the converse also holds.

Proof ▶

Rewrite the off-diagonal mass as the difference between the total mass of \(A\) and the diagonal overlap in the strong self-consistency inequality.

Lemma 3.23 Strong self-consistency and state-dependent distance
#

Uses

  • Definition 3.21
  • Definition 3.10

Lean declarations

  • MIPStarRE.LDT.Preliminaries.twoNotionsOfSelfConsistency

If \(A\) is \(\delta \)-strongly self-consistent, then

\[ A_a^x \otimes I \approx _{2\delta } I \otimes A_a^x. \]

If \(A\) is projective, the converse also holds.

Proof ▶

Expand the squared norm of \((A_a^x \otimes I - I \otimes A_a^x)\lvert \psi \rangle \) and compare it with the diagonal overlap in the definition of strong self-consistency.

Lemma 3.24 Post-processing preserves strong self-consistency
#

Uses

  • Definition 3.5
  • Definition 3.21
  • Lemma 3.23

If \(A\) is \(\delta \)-strongly self-consistent and \(f\) is a function on its answer set, then

\[ A_{[f(a)=b]}^x \otimes I \approx _{2\delta } I \otimes A_{[f(a)=b]}^x. \]
Proof ▶

The post-processed diagonal overlap dominates the original diagonal overlap, so the same strong self-consistency bound applies. Then use Lemma 3.23.

Lemma 3.25 Completeness transfer for a strongly self-consistent approximation
#

Uses

  • Definition 3.21
  • Definition 3.10

Let \(A\) be a \(\delta \)-strongly self-consistent submeasurement and let \(B\) be a submeasurement such that \(A_a^x \otimes I \approx _\varepsilon B_a^x \otimes I\). Then

\[ \langle \psi \rvert B \otimes I \lvert \psi \rangle \ge \langle \psi \rvert A \otimes I \lvert \psi \rangle - \delta - 2\sqrt\varepsilon . \]
Proof ▶

Bound the completeness of \(B\) from below by its diagonal overlap with \(A\), then replace that overlap by the diagonal overlap of \(A\) with itself.

Lemma 3.26 Self-consistency implies data-processing closeness
#

Uses

  • Definition 3.5
  • Definition 3.21
  • Definition 3.10
  • Lemma 3.24

Let \(A\) be a \(\delta \)-strongly self-consistent submeasurement and let \(P\) be a projective submeasurement such that \(P_a^x \otimes I \approx _\varepsilon A_a^x \otimes I\). Then for every function \(f\),

\[ P_{[f(a)=b]}^x \otimes I \approx _{8\delta + 8\sqrt\varepsilon } A_{[f(a)=b]}^x \otimes I. \]
Proof ▶

First compare the post-processed family of \(P\) to \(I \otimes A_{[f(a)=b]}^x\), using strong self-consistency of \(A\) and closeness of \(P\) to \(A\). Then move back to the original side of the bipartition and compose the two bounds.

Lemma 3.27 Self-consistency moves one copy to the same side
#

Uses

  • Definition 3.21

Let \(A=\{ A_a\} \) be a \(\zeta \)-strongly self-consistent submeasurement. Then

\[ \sum _a \langle \psi \rvert A_a^2 \otimes I \lvert \psi \rangle \ge \sum _a \langle \psi \rvert A_a \otimes I \lvert \psi \rangle - \zeta . \]
Proof ▶

Apply Cauchy–Schwarz to the two-sided overlap \(\sum _a \langle \psi \rvert A_a \otimes A_a \lvert \psi \rangle \) and compare it with the strong self-consistency lower bound.

Lemma 3.28 Bounding the missing mass of a close submeasurement
#

Uses

  • Definition 3.21
  • Definition 3.10
  • Lemma 3.27

Let \(A=\{ A_a\} \) be a measurement that is \(\zeta \)-strongly self-consistent, and let \(B=\{ B_a\} \) be a submeasurement such that \(A_a \otimes I \approx _\delta B_a \otimes I\). Writing \(B=\sum _a B_a\), one has

\[ \lVert (I-B) \otimes I\lvert \psi \rangle \rVert ^2 \le 2\sqrt\delta + \zeta . \]
Proof ▶

Since \(0 \le I-B \le I\), the left-hand side is at most \(1-\langle \psi \rvert B \otimes I \lvert \psi \rangle \). Compare \(\sum _a \langle \psi \rvert B_a^2 \otimes I \lvert \psi \rangle \) with \(\sum _a \langle \psi \rvert A_a B_a \otimes I \lvert \psi \rangle \) and then with \(\sum _a \langle \psi \rvert A_a^2 \otimes I \lvert \psi \rangle \) using the \(\approx _\delta \) hypothesis, and finish with Lemma 3.27.

Lemma 3.29 Completing a close submeasurement to a full measurement
#

Uses

  • Definition 3.21
  • Definition 3.10
  • Lemma 3.14
  • Lemma 3.28

Lean declarations

  • MIPStarRE.LDT.Preliminaries.completingToMeasurement

Let \(A=\{ A_a\} \) be a measurement that is \(\zeta \)-strongly self-consistent, let \(B=\{ B_a\} \) be a submeasurement such that \(A_a \otimes I \approx _\delta B_a \otimes I\), and let \(C\) be the measurement obtained by adding the missing mass \(I-B\) to one distinguished answer \(a^*\). Then

\[ A_a \otimes I \approx _{2\delta + 4\sqrt\delta + 2\zeta } C_a \otimes I. \]
Proof ▶

By the squared triangle inequality,

\[ \sum _a \lVert (A_a-C_a)\otimes I\lvert \psi \rangle \rVert ^2 \le 2\delta + 2\lVert (I-B) \otimes I\lvert \psi \rangle \rVert ^2. \]

Lemma 3.28 gives

\[ \lVert (I-B) \otimes I\lvert \psi \rangle \rVert ^2 \le 2\sqrt\delta + \zeta , \]

and substituting this yields the claimed bound.
