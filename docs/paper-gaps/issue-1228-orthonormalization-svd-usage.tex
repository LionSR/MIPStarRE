\section{The source step}

We review the singular value decomposition used in the orthonormalization proof
of \cite{Ji2020LowIndividualDegree}.%
\footnote{The SVD passage is in
\path{references/ldt-paper/orthonormalization.tex}, lines~774--845.  The
identities derived from it are in lines~862--896 and 984--1064, and the final
$P$-versus-$Q$ estimate is in lines~1089--1192.}
This is a local linear-algebra step.  It is separate from the earlier spectral
truncation of $A_a$ and the choice of orthonormal bases for projector ranges.

After rounding and rank reduction, the paper has projectors $Q_a$ with
$Q=\sum_aQ_a$ and $\sum_a\operatorname{rank}(Q_a)\le d$.  Choosing an
orthonormal basis $\ket{v_{a,i}}$ for the range of each $Q_a$, and an auxiliary
basis $\ket{a,i}$, it defines
\[
  X_a=\sum_i \ket{a,i}\bra{v_{a,i}},
  \qquad
  X=\sum_a X_a,
  \tag{\texttt{[blueprint] def:matrix-decomposition-Q}}
\]
and a projective measurement $T=\{T_a\}$ on the auxiliary space.  The basic
identities are
\[
  X_a=T_aX,
  \qquad
  Q_a=X_a^\dagger X_a=X^\dagger T_aX.
  \tag{\texttt{lem:xa-t}, \texttt{lem:qa-restated}}
\]
The SVD is then applied to the rectangular $m\times d$ matrix $X$, where
$m=\sum_a\operatorname{rank}(Q_a)\le d$:
\[
  X=U\Sigma_{m\times d}V^\dagger.
  \tag{\texttt{[blueprint] def:svd-of-X}}
\]

\section{What the SVD supplies}

The paper uses the SVD to replace the singular values of $X$ by $1$.  It defines
\[
  \widehat X=UI_{m\times d}V^\dagger,
  \qquad
  P_a=\widehat X^\dagger T_a\widehat X.
  \tag{\texttt{[blueprint] def:projective-P}}
\]
Only three consequences are used later.

First, because $m\le d$,
\[
  \widehat X\widehat X^\dagger=I_{m\times m}.
  \tag{\texttt{lem:X-hat-squared}}
\]
This makes $P=\{P_a\}$ a projective submeasurement: inserting the identity
between $T_a$ and $T_b$ gives $P_aP_b=\mathbf 1_{a=b}P_a$.

Second,
\[
  X^\dagger\widehat X=\sqrt Q.
  \tag{\texttt{lem:X-times-X-hat}}
\]
Indeed, $X^\dagger\widehat X=V\Sigma_{d\times d}V^\dagger$ while
$X^\dagger X=Q=V\Sigma_{d\times d}^2V^\dagger$, and the middle operator is
positive.

Third,
\[
  (X-\widehat X)(X-\widehat X)^\dagger
  \le (XX^\dagger-I_{m\times m})^2.
  \tag{\texttt{lem:squared-difference}}
\]
This is the diagonal inequality $(\Sigma-I)^2\le(\Sigma^2-I)^2$ for
nonnegative singular values.  The proof then uses this estimate in one
Cauchy--Schwarz step to replace $X$ by $\widehat X$ in a $Q_aP_a$ overlap.  With
the almost-projectivity bound for $Q$, it obtains
\[
  Q_a\ot I\approx_{30\zeta^{1/4}}P_a\ot I,
  \tag{\texttt{lem:P-Q-approx}}
\]
and hence, using the rank-reduction estimate
$A_a\ot I\approx_{12\sqrt\zeta}Q_a\ot I$, the final bound
$A_a\ot I\approx_{84\zeta^{1/4}}P_a\ot I$.

Thus the SVD is a polar-repair step: it keeps the right Gram operator
$X^\dagger X=Q$ and replaces $X$ by a coisometry $\widehat X$ with the same
positive square-root coupling to $Q$.

\section{Blueprint and formalization}

The blueprint follows the same structure.  It records the matrix decomposition,
the SVD step, and the definition of $P$ under the labels
\texttt{def:matrix-decomposition-Q}, \texttt{def:svd-of-X}, and
\texttt{def:projective-P}; it then records the three SVD consequences and the
$P$-versus-$Q$ estimate under the labels displayed above.%
\footnote{See \path{blueprint/src/chapter/ch04_projective.tex}, lines~704--785
and 833--1153.}

The Lean development separates packaging from mathematics.  The formal object
for the SVD stage is a reduced $X,\widehat X,P$ data package, not a full record
of $U$, $V$, and $\Sigma$.  This package stores exactly the identities needed
downstream: $Q_a=X^\dagger T_aX$, $\widehat X\widehat X^\dagger=I$, and
$X^\dagger\widehat X=\sqrt Q$.  The constructors that assemble this package are
mostly bookkeeping wrappers: they keep the paper's objects visible and prove
$X^\dagger X=Q$ from $Q_a=X^\dagger T_aX$ and $\sum_aT_a=I$.%
\footnote{The formal names are \leanid{svdOfX}, \leanid{QXPLayerData},
\leanid{QXPLayerData.ofQLayerAndSvdIdentities}, and related constructors in
\doclink{MIPStarRE/LDT/MakingMeasurementsProjective/QXPLayer/Core.lean}.}

The substantive local algebra has two forms.  One form proves the paper-facing
rectangular-SVD calculations once matrices $U,V,\Sigma,I_{m\times d}$ are
supplied.  The other form avoids an explicit SVD record: from $X^\dagger X=Q$,
positivity of $Q$, and $m\le d$, it constructs $\widehat X$ directly from the
positive spectral subspace of $Q$.  This positive-Gram construction gives the
same two identities for $\widehat X$ that the paper obtains from the SVD.%
\footnote{The SVD-adapter declarations include
\leanid{rectangularSvd\_xHat\_coisometry}, \leanid{rectangularSvd\_xHat\_mixed\_raw},
\leanid{rectangularSvd\_middle\_eq\_sqrt\_of\_square}, and
\leanid{exists\_xHat\_of\_rectangularSvd}.  The positive-Gram route includes
\leanid{exists\_xHat\_of\_positive\_gram\_spectrum},
\leanid{exists\_xHat\_of\_sigmaFinRangeEmbedding\_positiveGram}, and
\leanid{pQApprox\_ofRankReductionSigmaRangePositiveGram}.  These are in
\doclink{MIPStarRE/LDT/MakingMeasurementsProjective/QXPLayerIdentities.lean}.}

Mathlib supplies the finite-dimensional Hilbert-space and spectral ingredients:
orthonormal bases, adjoints, the Hermitian spectral theorem, matrix/euclidean
linear-map bridges, and the continuous-functional-calculus square root.  The
version of Mathlib pinned here does not currently provide a rectangular complex
SVD or polar decomposition theorem with exactly the output shape needed here.
The project-local positive-Gram route fills that gap; possible upstreaming of
the generic pieces is tracked separately.%
\footnote{Representative reused APIs include \leanid{Orthonormal},
\leanid{Matrix.IsHermitian.eigenvectorBasis}, \leanid{CFC.sqrt}, and
\leanid{CFC.sqrt\_unique}.  The local coisometry helper
\leanid{Matrix.exists\_mul\_conjTranspose\_eq\_one\_of\_card\_le} is in
\doclink{MIPStarRE/Quantum/FiniteHilbert.lean}.  Upstreaming is tracked in

\section{Conclusion}

There is no mathematical discrepancy in the paper's SVD step.  The paper uses
SVD only to construct $\widehat X$ with
$\widehat X\widehat X^\dagger=I$ and $X^\dagger\widehat X=\sqrt Q$, and then to
prove the squared-difference estimate used in the $P$-versus-$Q$ bound.  The
blueprint and Lean formalization preserve exactly this mechanism.

The current formalization is more modular than the paper: it stores the two
primitive $\widehat X$ identities and can produce them either from explicit
rectangular-SVD data or from a positive-Gram construction using Mathlib spectral
APIs.  Remaining orthonormalization work concerns other inputs, such as spectral
truncation and locality-preserving repair; it is not a new SVD gap.
