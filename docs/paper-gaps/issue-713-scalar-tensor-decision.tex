\section{The assertion}

This note concerns the Schwartz--Zippel marginalization step in the proof
of the commutativity theorem (\Cref{thm:com-main}) in
\cite{Ji2020LowIndividualDegree}. The paper's proof
(\texttt{commutativity-G.tex}, lines~345--385) works on the tensor form
\[
  \E_{\bx,\by} \sum_{g,h}
    \bigl\langle \psi \bigm|
      G^{\by}_h G^{\bx}_g G^{\by}_h \otimes G^{\bx}_g
    \bigm| \psi \bigr\rangle,
  \tag{\texttt{eq:gcom4-diff}}
\]
which is manifestly positive semidefinite (PSD) per summand because each
term factors as $V^\dagger V$ with
$V = (G^{\bx}_g G^{\by}_h) \otimes \sqrt{G^{\bx}_g}$. The outer absolute
value therefore drops, and the Schwartz--Zippel collision bound applies
directly to the off-diagonal terms $g \neq g'$.

The Lean formalization uses instead the scalar form
\[
  \E_{\bx,\by} \sum_{g,h}
    \bigl\langle \psi \bigm|
      G^{\bx}_g G^{\by}_h G^{\bx}_g G^{\by}_h \otimes I
    \bigm| \psi \bigr\rangle,
\]
and the corresponding evaluated-slice scalar averages
\texttt{fullSliceABAAvg}, \texttt{fullSliceABABAvg},
\texttt{evaluatedSliceABAAvg}, \texttt{evaluatedSliceABABAvg}
defined in \texttt{FullSlice.lean}.\footnote{\texttt{MIPStarRE/LDT/Commutativity/Transport/FullSlice.lean},
  lines~282, 296, 314, 325.}

\section{The point at issue}

The paper's argument for bounding the difference
(\texttt{eq:gcom4-diff}) between full-polynomial and evaluated forms
exploits the PSD property of the tensor split $G^{\by}_h G^{\bx}_g
G^{\by}_h \otimes G^{\bx}_g$. Each off-diagonal term
$g \neq g'$ contributes
\[
  \E_{\bu} \mathbf 1[g(\bu) = g'(\bu)]
  \cdot
  \bigl\langle \psi \bigm|
    G^{\by}_h G^{\bx}_g G^{\by}_h \otimes G^{\bx}_{g'}
  \bigm| \psi \bigr\rangle,
\]
and the PSD sandwich guarantees that the second factor is non-negative.
Summing gives a total of at most $dm/q$, the Schwartz--Zippel parameter.

When the same argument is attempted in the scalar form
$G^{\bx}_g G^{\by}_h G^{\bx}_{g'} G^{\by}_h \otimes I$, the off-diagonal
term becomes
\[
  \E_{\bu} \mathbf 1[g(\bu) = g'(\bu)]
  \cdot
  \bigl\langle \psi \bigm|
    G^{\bx}_g G^{\by}_h G^{\bx}_{g'} G^{\by}_h \otimes I
  \bigm| \psi \bigr\rangle.
\]
The scalar sandwich $G^{\bx}_g G^{\by}_h G^{\bx}_{g'} G^{\by}_h$ is
\emph{not} PSD for $g \neq g'$: it is a product of
Hermitian matrices that need not have a sign. The scalar collision term
can be negative, so the outer absolute value cannot be dropped by the
same nonnegativity argument.

\section{The hybrid resolution (Option~3)}

The Lean development adopts a hybrid strategy
(Issue~\#713, Option~3).\footnote{See \texttt{MIPStarRE/LDT/Commutativity/Main/Auxiliary.lean},
  lines~31--60, for the triangle lemma \texttt{abs\_sub\_le\_of\_tensor\_triangle}
  that implements the hybrid bound.}
The scalar-to-tensor bridges are proved internally using
\texttt{closenessOfIP} (\Cref{prop:closeness-of-ip}):
\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{fullSliceABAAvg} $\approx_{\sqrt\zeta}$
    \texttt{fullSliceBABAtensorAvg} (private, at line~341 of
    \texttt{FullSlice.lean});
  \item \texttt{fullSliceABABAvg} $\approx_{\sqrt\zeta}$
    \texttt{fullSliceABABtensorAvg} (private, at line~393);
  \item analogous bridges for the evaluated-slice averages.
\end{itemize}
These bridges cost $\sqrt\zeta$, where $\zeta$ is the strong
self-consistency parameter, because they use \texttt{closenessOfIP}
to move one tensor factor from the left register to the right register.

The Schwartz--Zippel marginalization is then performed in the tensor
form (where the PSD argument applies cleanly), and the result is
transported back to the scalar form. The round trip adds $2\sqrt\zeta$
to the error budget compared to the paper's direct tensor route. The
combined bound becomes
\[
  \bigl|
    \text{full-slice scalar} - \text{evaluated-slice scalar}
  \bigr|
  \le \frac{md}{q} + 2\sqrt\zeta,
\]
instead of the paper's $md/q$.

The extra $2\sqrt\zeta$ is absorbed into the final commutativity error
parameter $\nu = 30m(\gamma^{1/4} + \zeta^{1/4} + (d/q)^{1/4})$ in
\Cref{thm:com-main}, and similarly into the intermediate
\texttt{commDataProcessedGError} constant $48m(\sqrt\gamma + \sqrt\zeta)$.
Since $\nu$ is already $\poly(m)$ times fractional powers of the error
parameters, the additional $2\sqrt\zeta$ term does not change the theorem
statement or its asymptotic form.

\section{Conclusion}

The scalar public API is retained because it is the natural form in which
the paper states the overall commutativity theorem, and because the tensor
form would require additional structure (right-register tensor factors)
that is not directly available from the strategy definitions. The
mathematical price is an extra $2\sqrt\zeta$ in the intermediate
full-slice-to-evaluated-slice bound, which is absorbed harmlessly into
the final error parameter.

\noindent\textbf{Verdict:} harmless correction. The $2\sqrt\zeta$
additional cost does not alter the final theorem's error parameter or its
logical validity, because the relevant error budget already accommodates
terms of this magnitude. The paper's PSD argument for the tensor form
remains the mathematically cleaner route, but the scalar hybrid is a
legitimate formalization substitute.
