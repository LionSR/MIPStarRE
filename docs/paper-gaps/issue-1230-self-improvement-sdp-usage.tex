\section{The source SDP}

The self-improvement lemma in \cite{Ji2020LowIndividualDegree} uses one SDP.%
\footnote{The SDP is in \path{references/ldt-paper/self_improvement.tex},
lines~62--88.  The strict-feasibility and complementary-slackness paragraph is
in lines~168--190.}
For each low individual degree polynomial $g$, set
\[
  A_g=\E_{\bu\sim\F_q^m} A^{\bu}_{g(\bu)}.
\]
The primal and dual programs are
\begin{align}
  \sup_{\{T_g\}}&\quad \sum_g\tr(T_gA_g) \\
  \text{subject to}&\quad T_g\ge0,\\[-0.2em]
  &\quad \sum_gT_g\le I,
  \tag{\texttt{eq:primal-objective}}
\end{align}
\begin{align}
  \inf_Z&\quad \tr(Z) \\
  \text{subject to}&\quad Z\ge A_g\quad\text{for every }g.
  \tag{\texttt{eq:dual-objective}}
\end{align}
The primal variable is a \textbf{submeasurement}.  The SDP lemma then chooses an
optimal pair satisfying
\[
  \sum_gT_g=I,
  \qquad
  T_gZ=T_gA_g\quad\text{for every }g.
  \tag{\texttt{lem:sdp}, \texttt{eq:slater}}
\]
The proof uses both layers: feasibility gives $\sum_gT_g\le I$, while the chosen
optimum gives the saturated identity and complementary slackness.

\section{How the proof uses the optimum}

The paper defines
\[
  H^u_h=A^u_{h(u)}T_hA^u_{h(u)},
  \qquad
  H_h=\E_{\bu}H^{\bu}_h.
  \tag{\texttt{self\_improvement.tex:203--220}}
\]
The SDP contributes three facts.

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Submeasurement bounds.}  The inequalities $T_g\ge0$ and
  $\sum_gT_g\le I$ imply that $H^u$ and $H$ are submeasurements.
  \item \textbf{Saturation.}  The identity $\sum_gT_g=I$ lets sums over $T_g$
  collapse to the identity.
  \item \textbf{Slackness and boundedness.}  The identity $T_gZ=T_gA_g$ lets the
  proof replace $A_g$ by $Z$ after left multiplication by $T_g$:
  \[
    \E_{\bu}\sum_h\bra{\psi}(T_hA^{\bu}_{h(\bu)})\ot I\ket{\psi}
    =\sum_h\bra{\psi}(T_hZ)\ot I\ket{\psi}.
  \]
  Dual feasibility $Z\ge A_g$ then supplies the boundedness certificate carried
  through the induction.%
  \footnote{The two slackness substitutions are in
  \path{references/ldt-paper/self_improvement.tex}, lines~397--400 and 580--584.
  The boundedness conclusion is restated in
  \path{references/ldt-paper/inductive_step.tex}, lines~277--284, 315--322,
  477--484, and 542--549.}
\end{enumerate}

\section{Key theorem forms to develop}

The formal SDP work should isolate the following mathematical theorems.

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Finite-dimensional SDP duality with Slater.}  For a Hermitian
  objective $C$, a real linear constraint map $\mathcal A$, and right-hand side
  $b$, strict primal and dual feasibility should imply optimizers $X_*$ and
  $y_*$ for
  \[
    \sup\{\langle C,X\rangle: \mathcal A(X)=b,\ X\succeq0\},
    \qquad
    \inf\{\langle b,y\rangle: \mathcal A^*(y)-C\succeq0\},
  \]
  equality of the two values, and complementary slackness
  $X_*(\mathcal A^*(y_*)-C)=0$.

  \item \textbf{Paper SDP equivalence.}  The canonical block variable should be
  equivalent to effects $T_g$ plus a slack block $S$, with
  $\sum_gT_g+S=I$ and the same objective value $\sum_g\tr(T_gA_g)$.

  \item \textbf{Self-improvement output.}  Specializing the duality theorem to
  the paper's $A_g$ should produce $T$ and $Z$ with
  \[
    T_g\ge0,\qquad \sum_gT_g=I,\qquad Z\ge A_g,\qquad T_gZ=T_gA_g.
  \]

  \item \textbf{Interface bridge.}  The matrix-level witness should imply the
  abstract self-improvement SDP statement consumed by the helper proof, including
  any extra dominance field required by the current reduced interface.%
  \footnote{This bridge is already isolated in
  \doclink{MIPStarRE/LDT/SelfImprovement/Theorems/Results/SdpMatrixBridge.lean}.}
\end{enumerate}

\section{Blueprint and formalization}

The blueprint follows the paper: it states the submeasurement primal, records the
strict feasible witness $T_g=(2M)^{-1}I$, and separately records the
slackness-producing optimal witness.%
\footnote{See \path{blueprint/src/chapter/ch07_self_improvement.tex}, especially
\texttt{lem:sdp-uniform-feasible-witness},
\texttt{lem:sdp-matrix-feasible-bounds},
\texttt{lem:sdp-matrix-slackness-output}, and \texttt{lem:sdp}.}

The Lean development already keeps the submeasurement boundary explicit.  The
primal objective, strict primal witness, slackness equation, and sandwiched
construction all take a submeasurement as input.  The remaining open task is the
producer for the stronger slackness-carrying statement.%
\footnote{Representative declarations are \leanid{sdpStrictPrimalSubMeas},

\leanid{averagedSandwichedPolynomialSubMeas} in
\doclink{MIPStarRE/LDT/SelfImprovement/Defs.lean}.  The reduced and strengthened
interfaces are \leanid{SdpStatement} and \leanid{SdpStatementWithSlackness} in
\doclink{MIPStarRE/LDT/SelfImprovement/Theorems/Statements.lean}.  The open

\section{Conclusion}

There is \textbf{no new mathematical discrepancy} in how the LDT paper uses the
SDP.  The paper uses a submeasurement primal program, then derives saturation
and complementary slackness for a chosen optimum.  Those derived facts are what
the self-improvement proof needs.

The remaining work is \textbf{hard formalization work}: prove or import the
finite-dimensional SDP duality theorem with complementary slackness, specialize
it to the paper's block program, and connect the resulting witness to the
self-improvement interface.
