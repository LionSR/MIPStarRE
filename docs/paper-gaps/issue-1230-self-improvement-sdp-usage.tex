\section{The source step}

We review the semidefinite program used in the self-improvement lemma of the
low individual degree test paper \cite{Ji2020LowIndividualDegree}.%
\footnote{The SDP is in \path{references/ldt-paper/self_improvement.tex},
lines~62--88.  The strict feasibility and complementary-slackness paragraph is
in lines~168--190.  The downstream helper proof starts at line~194.}
This is the only SDP used as an analytic ingredient in the paper.  Its job is to
choose a polynomial-indexed primal family $T=\{T_g\}$ and a dual certificate
$Z$.

For a low individual degree polynomial $g$, set
\[
  A_g=\E_{\bu\sim\F_q^m} A^{\bu}_{g(\bu)}.
\]
The paper's primal and dual programs are
\begin{align}
  \sup_{\{T_g\}}
    &\quad \sum_g \tr(T_gA_g) \\
  \text{subject to}
    &\quad T_g\ge 0,\qquad \sum_gT_g\le I,
  \tag{\texttt{eq:primal-objective}}
\end{align}
\begin{align}
  \inf_Z
    &\quad \tr(Z) \\
  \text{subject to}
    &\quad Z\ge A_g\quad\text{for every }g.
  \tag{\texttt{eq:dual-objective}}
\end{align}
Thus the primal feasible object is a submeasurement, not a measurement.  The SDP
lemma then selects an optimal pair satisfying the additional conclusions
\[
  \sum_gT_g=I,
  \qquad
  T_gZ=T_gA_g\quad\text{for every }g.
  \tag{\texttt{lem:sdp}, \texttt{eq:slater}}
\]
The equality $\sum_gT_g=I$ is not a primal constraint.  It is a property of the
chosen optimum.

\section{How the proof uses the optimum}

After fixing the optimal pair $(T,Z)$, the paper defines
\[
  H^u_h=A^u_{h(u)}T_hA^u_{h(u)},
  \qquad
  H_h=\E_{\bu}H^{\bu}_h.
  \tag{\texttt{self\_improvement.tex:203--220}}
\]
The proof uses three SDP outputs.

First, the submeasurement inequalities $T_g\ge0$ and $\sum_gT_g\le I$ make each
$H^u$ and the average $H$ submeasurements.  This is the feasibility part of the
primal SDP.

Second, the selected optimum is saturated: $\sum_gT_g=I$.  The proof uses this
when sums over $T_g$ collapse to the identity.

Third, complementary slackness lets the proof replace $A_g$ by $Z$ after left
multiplication by $T_g$.  In the completeness argument, for example,
\[
  \E_{\bu}\sum_h\bra{\psi}(T_hA^{\bu}_{h(\bu)})\ot I\ket{\psi}
  =\sum_h\bra{\psi}(T_hA_h)\ot I\ket{\psi}
  =\sum_h\bra{\psi}(T_hZ)\ot I\ket{\psi}.
\]
The same replacement is used again in the strong self-consistency argument.%
\footnote{The two swaps are in
\path{references/ldt-paper/self_improvement.tex}, lines~397--400 and 580--584.}
Dual feasibility $Z\ge A_g$ then supplies the boundedness certificate carried
through the induction.%
\footnote{The boundedness conclusion is restated in
\path{references/ldt-paper/inductive_step.tex}, lines~277--284, 315--322,
477--484, and 542--549.}

\section{The submeasurement boundary}

The distinction between feasibility and saturation is essential.  If
$M=|\polyfunc{m}{q}{d}|$, the paper's strict Slater witness is
\[
  T_g=\frac{1}{2M}I,
  \qquad
  Z=2I.
\]
Its total primal mass is $\sum_gT_g=\frac12I$.  It is strictly feasible for the
paper's submeasurement SDP, but it would be excluded by an equality-constrained
primal program.

This was the mismatch behind the earlier SDP weakening issue: the formal primal
interface must stay at $\sum_gT_g\le I$ until the strong-duality argument
selects a saturated optimum.%
\footnote{The original mismatch is documented in
\path{docs/paper-gaps/issue-196-sdp-weakening.tex}.}

\section{Blueprint and formalization}

The blueprint follows the paper.  It states the primal with $\sum_gT_g\le I$,
records the strict feasible witness $T_g=(2M)^{-1}I$, and separately records
the matrix-level slackness output giving a complete primal measurement and the
equations $T_gZ=T_gA_g$.%
\footnote{See \path{blueprint/src/chapter/ch07_self_improvement.tex}, especially
\texttt{lem:sdp-uniform-feasible-witness},
\texttt{lem:sdp-matrix-feasible-bounds},
\texttt{lem:sdp-matrix-slackness-output}, and \texttt{lem:sdp}.}

The Lean development mirrors this split.  The primal objective, the strict
primal witness, the complementary-slackness equation, and the sandwiched
construction all take a submeasurement as input.  A reduced SDP statement records
only the facts currently produced by the wrapper: a submeasurement whose total is
$I$, a positive dual operator, and dual feasibility.  A stronger statement adds
the complementary-slackness equations required by the paper-facing helper
argument.%
\footnote{Representative declarations are \leanid{sdpStrictPrimalSubMeas},
\leanid{sdpPrimalObjective}, \leanid{sdpComplementarySlacknessEquation}, and
\leanid{averagedSandwichedPolynomialSubMeas} in
\doclink{MIPStarRE/LDT/SelfImprovement/Defs.lean}, and \leanid{SdpStatement}
and \leanid{SdpStatementWithSlackness} in
\doclink{MIPStarRE/LDT/SelfImprovement/Theorems/Statements.lean}.}
At the matrix layer, the strong-duality shape is represented by a witness with
identity total, dual feasibility, equality of primal and dual objective values,
and complementary slackness.%
\footnote{See \leanid{MatrixSdpOptimalWitness} and
\leanid{MatrixSdpStatementWithSlackness} in
\doclink{MIPStarRE/LDT/SelfImprovement/MatrixRealization.lean}.  The open task
of proving the slackness-carrying producer is \ghissue{1230}.}

\section{Conclusion}

There is no new mathematical discrepancy in how the paper uses the SDP.  The
paper uses a submeasurement primal program, then derives saturation and
complementary slackness for a chosen optimum.  Those derived facts are exactly
what the self-improvement proof uses to build $H$ and carry the boundedness
certificate $Z$.

The formalization now records the submeasurement boundary correctly.  The
remaining work is to formalize the strong-duality and complementary-slackness
producer for the stronger SDP statement.  That is a formal proof task, not a
change to the paper's SDP argument.
