\section{The paper statement}

Theorem~\emph{Self-improvement} (both the standalone version in
\texttt{self\_improvement.tex} and the induction-section version in
\texttt{inductive\_step.tex}) assumes the following hypothesis on the
input measurement $G \in \polymeas{m}{q}{d}$:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item (Consistency with~$A$): On average over $\bu \sim \F_q^m$,
  \begin{equation*}
    A_a^{\bu} \ot I \simeq_\nu  I \ot G_{[g(\bu)=a]}.
  \end{equation*}
\end{enumerate}
Under this hypothesis the paper proves completeness
$\bra{\psi} H \ot I \ket{\psi} \ge (1-\nu)-\zeta$, point
consistency at~$\zeta$, strong self-consistency at~$\zeta$, and the
boundedness estimate $\bra{\psi} Z \ot (I-H) \ket{\psi} \le
\zeta$, where
$\zeta = 3000 m (\eps^{1/32} + \delta^{1/32} + (d/q)^{1/32})$.
For the non-projective helper lemma the error is
$\widehat\zeta = 100 m (\eps^{1/2} + \delta^{1/2} + (d/q)^{1/2})$.

\section{The Lean formalization}

The Lean theorem
\leanid{MIPStarRE.LDT.SelfImprovement.selfImprovement}
(\path{SelfImprovement/Theorems/Results.lean}, lines~172--262) does \emph{not}
include the $\simeq_\nu$ consistency hypothesis. Instead it takes three
explicit bridge-input hypotheses:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \leanid{hhelperStrongSelfConsistency}~:
    \leanid{HelperStrongSelfConsistencyInput}
  \item \leanid{horthonormalization}~:
    \leanid{OrthonormalizationInput}
  \item \leanid{hfinalFields}~:
    \leanid{FinalFieldsInput params strategy eps delta nu}
\end{itemize}
The parameter \leanid{nu} appears in the conclusion
(\leanid{completeness} at level \leanid{(1 - nu) - selfImprovementError})
but is nowhere constrained by a hypothesis linking it to the input
measurement~\leanid{G}. Moreover \leanid{G} is passed to
\leanid{selfImprovementHelper} as an explicit unused argument
(\texttt{\_G}).

The induction-section wrapper
\leanid{MIPStarRE.LDT.MainInductionStep.selfImprovementInInductionSection}
(\path{MainInductionStep/Theorems.lean}, lines~66--155) \emph{does} list the
$\simeq_\nu$ hypothesis as the parameter \texttt{\_hcons}, but the proof
immediately delegates to \leanid{SelfImprovement.selfImprovementFromSubMeas},
which ignores it. The hypothesis is therefore an unused vestigial parameter
in the induction-section wrapper as well.

\section{Why this matters}

The paper's proof of the four self-improvement conclusions chains through
$G$'s $\nu$-consistency with~$A$; without this hypothesis the
completeness bound $(1-\nu)-\zeta$ is logically correct but vacuous
(one may choose $\nu = 1$). The bridge-input system
(\leanid{SelfImprovementBridgeInputs}, \leanid{HelperStrongSelfConsistencyInput},
\leanid{FinalFieldsInput}) is an explicit documented workaround that
packages the missing proof obligations, but it does \emph{not} restore
the paper's $\simeq_\nu$ hypothesis to the statement of the Lean
\leanid{selfImprovement} theorem.

\section{Blueprint status}

The blueprint entry for Theorem~\emph{Self-improvement}
(\path{ch07\_self\_improvement.tex}, lines~490--495) lists four Lean
declarations---\leanid{selfImprovement},
\leanid{SelfImprovementBridgeInputs},
\leanid{selfImprovementFromBridgeInputs}, and
\leanid{selfImprovementFromBridgeInputsSubMeas}---and marks them
\leanid{\textbackslash leanok}. While all four declarations compile without
\leanid{sorry}, the paper statement and the Lean statement differ in
their hypotheses. This gap is tracked by \ghissue{931} (self-improvement
input producers).

\section{Error coefficients}

All error constants match the paper when the bridge inputs are
discharged:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \leanid{selfImprovementHelperError}~$= 100 m (\eps^{1/2} + \delta^{1/2} + (d/q)^{1/2})$ --- matches $\widehat\zeta$;
  \item \leanid{selfImprovementOrthogonalizationError} delegates to \leanid{orthonormalizationError} --- the paper uses $100 \widehat\zeta^{\,1/4}$;
  \item \leanid{selfImprovementDataProcessingError}~$= 8 \widehat\zeta + 8 \sqrt{\widehat\zeta_{\text{ortho}}}$ --- matches the paper;
  \item \leanid{selfImprovementError}~$= 3000 m (\eps^{1/32} + \delta^{1/32} + (d/q)^{1/32})$ --- matches the paper's $\zeta$.
\end{itemize}

\section{Classification}

This is a \textbf{documented structural gap}: the discrepancy between
the paper statement and the Lean statement is explicitly surfaced by
the bridge-input system and tracked by~\ghissue{931}, but the
\leanid{\textbackslash leanok} annotation on the blueprint node
could mislead a reader into thinking the full paper theorem is
formalized without additional hypotheses.
