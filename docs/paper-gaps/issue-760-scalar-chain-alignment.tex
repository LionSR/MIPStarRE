\section{The assertion}

This note examines the evaluated-slice commutativity proof for
Lemma~\texttt{lem:comm-data-processed-g}\footnote{The paper label is
\path{lem:comm-data-processed-g} in
\path{references/ldt-paper/commutativity-G.tex}, lines 16--47.}
in \cite{Ji2020LowIndividualDegree}.
The lemma states that, under the three hypotheses
(consistency with $A$, strong self-consistency, and boundedness),
the $G$ measurements approximately commute after evaluation for
distinct point pairs. The conclusion is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:processed-conclusion}
  \mathbb{E}_{\bu,\bv,\bx,\by} \sum_{a,b}
    \|(G^{\bx}_{[g(\bu)=a]} G^{\by}_{[h(\bv)=b]}
      - G^{\by}_{[h(\bv)=b]} G^{\bx}_{[g(\bu)=a]})
    \otimes I | \psi\rangle\|^{2}
  \leq
  \nu ,\qquad
  \nu = 48m (\sqrt{\gamma} + \sqrt{\zeta}).
  \tag{\texttt{eq:processed-conclusion}}
\end{equation}

\section{The ten-step chain}

The paper's proof (commutativity-G.tex, lines~48--130) expands the
commutator square and isolates the $\ABAB$ term. The path from
$\mathbb{E}[\sum \ABAB]$ to $\mathbb{E}[\sum \ABA]$ passes through
ten approximation steps:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Apply $\closenessOfIP$ with $A = G^{\bu,\bx}_a \otimes I$ and
    $B = I \otimes A^{\bu,\bx}_a$ (cost $2\sqrt{\zeta}$);
  \item Swap $A^{\bv,\by}_b$ onto the right register
    (cost $\sqrt{\zeta}$, \texttt{clm:g-comm-stability});
  \item Insert Bob's second measurement via $\closenessOfIP$
    (cost $2\sqrt{\zeta}$);
  \item Apply point commutativity
    (cost $6\sqrt{\gamma(m+1)}$, \texttt{thm:commutativity-points});
  \item Remove the right-register $G^{\bx}$ total
    (cost $\sqrt{\zeta} + 6\sqrt{\gamma(m+1)}$,
     \texttt{clm:g-comm-stability2});
  \item Reverse the $\closenessOfIP$ insertion of $A$ on the left
    register (cost $4\sqrt{\zeta}$, steps~6--7 of the paper);
  \item Post-process $G$ measurement outcomes
    (cost $\sqrt{\zeta}$, eq:\texttt{new-fact-that-i-derived});
  \item Reverse the second-coordinate post-processing
    (cost $\sqrt{\zeta}$, eq:\texttt{new-fact-that-i-derived});
\end{enumerate}
The individual error terms sum to
$12\sqrt{\zeta} + 12\sqrt{\gamma(m+1)}$.
Multiplying by~$2$ (from the commutator-square expansion
eq:\texttt{eq:gcom8}) and simplifying $12\sqrt{m+1} \leq 24m$
gives $\nu = 48m(\sqrt{\gamma}+\sqrt{\zeta})$.

\section{The Lean formalization}

The Lean lemma \texttt{commDataProcessedG}\footnote{The full path is
\path{MIPStarRE.LDT.Commutativity.commDataProcessedG}, implemented in
\path{MIPStarRE/LDT/Commutativity/ScalarApproximation/ProcessedG.lean},
lines 1972--2004. The error parameter is
\path{commDataProcessedGError} defined in
\path{MIPStarRE/LDT/Commutativity/Scaffold/Core.lean} as
$48\,m\,(\gamma^{1/2} + \zeta^{1/2})$.}
replicates the same ten-step chain. Each paper step is recorded as a
named \texttt{hphase} block and the final assembly (\texttt{hassemble})
adds them with the factor~$2$ from the commutator-square expansion.
The total matches the paper exactly.

The one substep that deserves explicit mention is the transition from
the $\BAB$ term to the $\ABA$ term. In the paper (line~395) this
is justified by the projectivity of~$G$, which makes
$\mathbb{E}[\sum G^{\bx}_{[g(\bu)=a]} G^{\by}_{[h(\bv)=b]}]
= \mathbb{E}[\sum G^{\by}_{[h(\bv)=b]} G^{\bx}_{[g(\bu)=a]}]$,
an exact equality. The Lean proof uses the lemma
\path{evaluatedSliceCommutation\_avg\_swap\_terms}\footnote{In
\path{MIPStarRE/LDT/Commutativity/EvaluatedSliceCommutation/Averages.lean},
line~217.}
which proves
$\operatorname{avgOver}_{\mathcal{D}}(\operatorname{avgBAB})
= \operatorname{avgOver}_{\mathcal{D}}(\operatorname{avgABA})$
as an \emph{equality} (not an approximation), exactly matching the
paper's step.

\section{Conclusion}

The verdict is that
\href{https://github.com/LionSR/MIPStarRE/issues/760}{issue \#760}
records no mathematical divergence. The Lean ten-step chain follows
the paper's proof step-by-step, uses the same lemmas
(\texttt{closenessOfIP}, \texttt{commutativityPoints},
\texttt{gCommStability}), and achieves the same total error bound
$48m(\sqrt{\gamma}+\sqrt{\zeta})$. The swap identity
$\operatorname{avgBAB} = \operatorname{avgABA}$ is an exact equality in
both the paper and the formalization. This note therefore documents a
successful alignment, not a gap.
